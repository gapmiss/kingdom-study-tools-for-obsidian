# Kedge

**KEDGE**, _noun_ \[allied probably to cag and keg.\] A small anchor, used to keep a ship steady when riding in a harbor or river, and particularly at the turn of the tide, to keep her clear of her bower anchor, also to remove her from one part of a harbor to another, being carried out in a boat and let go, as in warping or kedging. \[Sometimes written kedger.\]

**KEDGE**, _verb transitive_ To warp, as a ship; to move by means of a _kedge_ as in a river.

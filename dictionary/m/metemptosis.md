# Metemptosis

**METEMP'TOSIS**, _noun_ \[Gr. after, and to fall.\] In chronology, the solar equation necessary to prevent the new moon from happening a day too late, or the suppression of the bissextile once in 134 years. The opposite to this is the proemptosis, or the addition of a day every 300 years, and another every 2400 years.

# Mast

**M'AST**, _noun_ A long, round piece of timber, elevated or designed to be raised perpendicularly or nearly so, on the keel of a ship or other vessel, to which the yards, sails and rigging are attached, and by which they are supported. A _mast_ is a single stick, formed from the trunk of a tree, or it consists of many pieces of timber united by iron bands. Masts are of several kinds, as the main-mast, fore-mast, mizzen-mast, top-mast, top-gallant-mast, etc.

**M'AST**, _noun_ The fruit of the oak and beech, or other forest trees; nuts; acorns. \[It has no plural.\]

# Machinery

**MACHINERY**, _noun_ A complicated work, or combination of mechanical powers in a work, designed to increase, regulate or apply motion and force; as the _machinery_ of a watch or other chronometer.

**1.** Machines in general. The _machinery_ of a cotton-mill is often moved by a single wheel.

**2.** In epic and dramatic poetry, superhuman beings introduced by the poet to solve difficulty, or perform some exploit which exceeds human power; or the word may signify the agency of such beings, as supposed deities, angels, demons and the like.

Nee Deus intersit, nisi dignus vindice nodus Incidit.

A deity is not to be introduced, unless a difficulty occurs that requires the intervention of a god.

The _machinery_ of Milton's Paradise Lost, consists of numerous superhuman personages. Pope's Rape of the Lock is rendered very interesting by the _machinery_ of sylphs.

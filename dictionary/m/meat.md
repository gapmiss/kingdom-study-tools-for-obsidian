# Meat

**MEAT**, _noun_

**1.** Food in general; any thing eaten for nourishment, either by man or beast.

And God said, Behold, I have given you every herb--to you it shall be for _meat_ [[Genesis 1#29|Genesis 1:29]].

Every moving thing that liveth, shall be _meat_ for you [[Genesis 9#3|Genesis 9:3]].

Thy carcass shall be _meat_ to all fowls of the air [[Deuteronomy 28#26|Deuteronomy 28:26]].

**2.** The flesh of animals used as food. This is now the more usual sense of the word. The _meat_ of carnivorous animals is tough, coarse and ill flavored. The _meat_ of herbivorous animals is generally palatable.

**3.** In Scripture, spiritual food; that which sustains and nourishes spiritual life or holiness.

My flesh is _meat_ indeed. [[John 6#27|John 6:27]].

**4.** Spiritual comfort; that which delights the soul.

My _meat_ is to do the will of him that sent me. [[John 4#8|John 4:8]].

**5.** Products of the earth proper for food. [[Habakkuk 3#17|Habakkuk 3:17]].

**6.** The more abstruse doctrines of the gospel, or mysteries of religion. [[Hebrews 5#12|Hebrews 5:12]].

**7.** Ceremonial ordinances. [[Hebrews 13#9|Hebrews 13:9]].

To sit at _meat_ to sit or recline at the table.

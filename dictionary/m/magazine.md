# Magazine

**MAGAZINE**, _noun_

**1.** A store of arms, ammunition or provisions; or the building in which such store is deposited. It is usually a public store or storehouse.

**2.** In ships of war, a close room in the hold, where the gunpowder is kept. Large ships have usually two magazines.

**3.** A pamphlet periodically published, containing miscellaneous papers or compositions. The first publication of this kind in England, was the Gentleman's _magazine_ which first appeared in 1731, under the name of Sylvanus Urban, by Edward Cave, and which is still continued.

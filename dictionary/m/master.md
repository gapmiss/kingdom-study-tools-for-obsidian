# Master

**M'ASTER**, _noun_ \[Latin magister, compounded of the root of magis, major, greater.\]

**1.** A man who rules, governs or directs either men or business. A man who owns slaves is their master; he who has servants is their master; he who has apprentices is their master; he who has apprentices is their _master_ as he has the government and direction of them. The man who superintends and directs any business, is _master_ or _master_ workman.

_O thou my friend, my genius, come along,_

Thou _master_ of the poet and the song.

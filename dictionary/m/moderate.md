# Moderate

**MOD'ERATE**, _adjective_ \[Latin moderatus, from moderor, to limit, from modus, a limit.\]

**1.** Literally, limited; restrained; hence, temperate; observing reasonable bounds in indulgence; as _moderate_ in eating or drinking, or in other gratifications.

**2.** Limited in quantity; not excessive or expensive. He keeps a _moderate_ table.

**3.** Restrained in passion, ardor or temper; not violent; as _moderate_ men of both parties.

**4.** Not extreme in opinion; as a _moderate_ Calvinist or Lutheran.

**5.** Placed between extremes; holding the mean or middle place; as reformation of a _moderate_ kind.

**6.** Temperate; not extreme, violent or rigorous; as _moderate_ weather; a _moderate_ winter; _moderate_ heat; a _moderate_ breeze of wind.

**7.** Of a middle rate; as men of _moderate_ abilities.

**8.** Not swift; as a _moderate_ walk.

**MOD'ERATE**, _verb transitive_ To restrain from excess of any kind; to reduce from a state of violence; to lessen; to allay; to repress; as, to _moderate_ rage, action, desires, etc.; to _moderate_ heat or wind.

**1.** To temper; to make temperate; to qualify.

By its astringent quality, it moderates the relaxing quality of warm water.

**MOD'ERATE**, _verb intransitive_ To become less violent, severe, rigorous or intense. The cold or winter usually moderates in March; the heat of summer moderates in September.

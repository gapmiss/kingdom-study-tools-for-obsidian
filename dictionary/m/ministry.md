# Ministry

**MIN'ISTRY**, _noun_ \[Latin ministerium.\] The office, duties or functions of a subordinate agent of any kind.

**1.** Agency; service; aid; interposition; instrumentality.

He directs the affairs of this world by the ordinary _ministry_ of second causes.

**2.** Ecclesiastical function; agency or service of a minister of the gospel or clergyman in the modern church, or of priests, apostles and evangelists in the ancient. [[Acts 1#17|Acts 1:17]]. [[Romans 12#7|Romans 12:7]]. [[2 Timothy 4#5|2 Timothy 4:5]]. [[Numbers 4#12|Numbers 4:12]].

**3.** Time of ministration; duration of the office of a minister, civil or ecclesiastical.

The war with France was during the _ministry_ of Pitt.

**4.** Persons who compose the executive government or the council of a supreme magistrate; the body of ministers of state.

**5.** Business; employment.

He abhorred the wicked _ministry_ of arms.

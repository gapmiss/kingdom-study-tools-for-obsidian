# Moil

**MOIL**, _verb transitive_ To daub; to make dirty. \[Little used.\]

**1.** To weary. \[See the next word.\]

**MOIL**, _verb intransitive_ \[Gr. labor, combat; to strive, to fight; Latin molior, and miles.\] To labor; to toil; to work with painful efforts.

Now he must _moil_ and drudge for one he loathes.

**MOIL**, _noun_ A spot. \[Not in use.\]

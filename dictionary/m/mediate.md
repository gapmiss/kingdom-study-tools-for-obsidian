# Mediate

**ME'DIATE**, _adjective_ \[Latin medius, middle.\] Middle; being between the two extremes.

Anxious we hover in a _mediate_ state.

**1.** Interposed; intervening; being between two objects.

Soon the _mediate_ clouds shall be dispelled.

**2.** Acting by means, or by an intervening cause or instrument. Thus we speak of _mediate_ and immediate cause of its motion; the oar with which a man rows a boat is the immediate cause of its motion; but the rower is the _mediate_ cause, acting by means of the oar.

**ME'DIATE**, _verb intransitive_ To interpose between parties, as the equal friend of each; to act indifferently between contending parties, with a view to reconciliation; to intercede. The prince that mediates between nations and prevents a war, is the benefactor of both parties.

**1.** To be between two. \[Little used.\]

**ME'DIATE**, _verb transitive_ To effect by mediation or interposition between parties; as, to _mediate_ a peace.

**1.** To limit by something in the middle. \[Not used.\]

# Molder

**MOLDER**, _noun_ He who molds or forms into shape.

**MOLDER**, _verb intransitive_

**1.** To turn to dust by natural decay; to crumble; to perish; to waste away by a gradual separation of the component particles, without the presence of water. In this manner, animal and vegetable substances _molder_ and so also do stones and shells.

When statues _molder_ and when arches fall.

**2.** To be diminished; to waste away gradually.

If he had sat still, the enemy's army would have moldered to nothing.

**MOLDER**, _verb transitive_ To turn to dust; to crumble; to waste.

Some felt the silent stroke of moldering age.

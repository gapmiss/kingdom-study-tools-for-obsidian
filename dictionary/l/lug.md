# Lug

**LUG**, _verb transitive_ \[See [[Pluck]].\]

**1.** To haul; to drag; to pull with force, as something heavy and moved with difficulty.

Howler lugs him still through hedges.

**2.** To carry or convey with labor.

They must divide the image among them, and so _lug_ off every one his share.

To _lug_ out, to draw a sword, in burlesque.

**LUG**, _verb intransitive_ To drag; to move heavily.

**LUG**, _noun_

**1.** A small fish.

**2.** In Scotland, an ear. _obsolete_

**3.** A pole or perch, a land-measure. _obsolete_

**4.** Something heavy to be drawn or carried. \[Vulgar.\]

# Lean

**LEAN**, _verb intransitive_ \[Gr., Latin clino.\]

**1.** To deviate or move from a straight or perpendicular line; or to be in a position thus deviating. We say, a column leans to the north or to the east; it leans to the right or left.

**2.** To incline or propend; to tend toward.

They delight rather to _lean_ to their old customs -

**3.** To bend or incline so as to rest on something; as, to _lean_ against a wall or a pillar; to _lean_ on the arm of another.

**4.** To bend; to be in a bending posture.

**LEAN**, _verb transitive_

**1.** To incline; to cause to _lean_

**2.** To conceal. \[Not in use.\]

**LEAN**, _adjective_ \[Latin lenis, and Eng. slender.\]

**1.** Wanting flesh; meager; not fat; as a _lean_ body; a _lean_ man or animal.

**2.** Not rich; destitute of good qualities; bare; barren; as _lean_ earth.

**3.** Low; poor; in opposition to rich or great; as a _lean_ action. \[Unusual.\]

**4.** Barren of thought; destitute of that which improves or entertains; jejune; as a _lean_ discourse or dissertation.

**LEAN**, _noun_ That part of flesh which consists of muscle without the fat.

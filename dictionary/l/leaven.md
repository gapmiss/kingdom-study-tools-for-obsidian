# Leaven

**LEAVEN**, _noun_ lev'n. \[Latin levo, Eng. to lift.\]

**1.** A mass of sour dough, which, mixed with a larger quantity of dough or paste, produces fermentation in it and renders it light. During the seven days of the passover, no _leaven_ was permitted to be in the houses of the Jews. [[Exodus 12#15|Exodus 12:15]].

**2.** Any thing which makes a general change in the mass. It generally means something which corrupts or depraves that with which it is mixed.

Beware of the _leaven_ of the Pharisees and of the Sadducees. [[Matthew 16#6|Matthew 16:6]].

**LEAVEN**, _verb transitive_ lev'n.

**1.** To excite fermentation in; to raise and make light, as dough or paste.

A little _leaven_ leaveneth the whole lump. [[1 Corinthians 5#6|1 Corinthians 5:6]].

**2.** to taint; to imbue.

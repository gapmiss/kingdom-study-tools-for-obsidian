# Laureate

**LAU'REATE**, _adjective_ \[Latin laureatus, from laurea, a laurel.\]

Decked or invested with laurel; as _laureate_ hearse.

Soft on her lap her _laureate_ son reclines.

Poet _laureate_ in Great Britain, an officer of the king's household, whose business is to compose an ode annually for the king's birthday, and for the new year. It is said this title was first given him in the time of Edward IV.

**LAU'REATE**, _verb transitive_ To honor with a degree in the university, and a present of a wreath of laurel.

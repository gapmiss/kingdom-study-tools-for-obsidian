# Little

**LIT'TLE**, _adjective_

**1.** Small in size or extent; not great or large; as a _little_ body; a _little_ animal; a _little_ piece of ground; a _little_ table; a _little_ book; a _little_ hill; a _little_ distance; a _little_ child.

**2.** Short in duration; as a _little_ time or season; a _little_ sleep.

**3.** Small in quantity or amount; as a _little_ hay or grass; a _little_ food; a _little_ sum; a _little_ light; a _little_ air or water.

**4.** Of small dignity, power or importance.

When thou wast _little_ in thy own sight, wast thou not made the head of the tribes? [[1 Samuel 15#17|1 Samuel 15:17]].

**5.** Of small force or effect; slight; inconsiderable; as _little_ attention or exertions; _little_ effort; _little_ care or diligence, _little_ weight.

**LIT'TLE**, _noun_

**1.** A small quantity or amount. He demanded much and obtained _little_ He had _little_ of his father's liberality.

**2.** A small space.

Much was in _little_ writ -

**3.** Any thing small, slight or of inconsiderable importance.

I view with anger and disdain.

How _little_ gives thee joy and pain.

**4.** Not much.

These they are fitted for, and _little_ else.

**LIT'TLE**, _adverb_

**1.** In a small degree; slightly; as, he is _little_ changed. It is a _little_ discolored.

**2.** Not much; in a small quantity or space of time. He sleeps _little_

**3.** In some degree; slightly; sometimes preceded by _adjective_ The liquor is a _little_ sour or astringent.

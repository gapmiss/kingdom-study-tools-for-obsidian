# Lengthy

**LENGTH'Y**, _adjective_ Being long or moderately long; not short; not brief; applied mostly to moral subjects, as to discourses, writings, arguments, proceedings, etc.; as a _lengthy_ sermon; a _lengthy_ dissertation; a _lengthy_ detail.

Lengthy period.

No ministerial act in France, in matters of judicial cognizance, is done without a process verbal, in which the facts are stated amidst a great deal of _lengthy_ formality, with a degree of minuteness, highly profitable to the verbalizing officers and to the revenue.

P. S. Murray has sent or will send a double copy of the Bride and Giaour; in the last one, some _lengthy_ additions; pray accept them, according to old customs.

Chalmers' Political Annals, in treating of South Carolina - is by no means as _lengthy_ as Mr. Hewitt's History.

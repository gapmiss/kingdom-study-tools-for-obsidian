# Lament

**LAMENT**', _verb intransitive_ \[Latin lamentor.\]

**1.** To mourn; to grieve; to weep or wail; to express sorrow.

Jeremiah lamented for Josiah. [[2 Chronicles 35#25|2 Chronicles 35:25]].

**2.** To regret deeply; to feel sorrow.

**LAMENT'**, _verb transitive_ To bewail; to mourn for; to bemoan; to deplore.

One laughed at follies, one lamented crimes.

**LAMENT'**, _noun_ \[Latin lamentum.\] Grief or sorrow expressed in complaints or cries; lamentation; a weeping.

Torment, and loud _lament_ and furious rage.

\[This noun is used chiefly or solely in poetry.\]

# Glorify

**GLO'RIFY**, _verb transitive_ \[Latin gloria and facio, to make.\]

**1.** To praise; to magnify and honor in worship; to ascribe honor to, in thought or words. [[Psalms 86#9|Psalms 86:9]]

God is glorified, when such his excellency, above all things, is with due admiration acknowledged.

**2.** To make glorious; to exalt to glory, or to celestial happiness.

Whom he justified, them he also glorified. [[Romans 8#1|Romans 8:1]].

The God of our fathers hath glorified his son

Jesus. [[Acts 3#1|Acts 3:1]].

**3.** To praise; to honor; to extol.

Whomsoever they find to be most licentious of life--him they set up and _glorify_

**4.** To procure honor or praise to.

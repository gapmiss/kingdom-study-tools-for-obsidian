# Gentle

**GEN'TLE**, _adjective_ \[See [[Genteel]].\] Well born; of a good family or respectable birth, though not noble; as the studies of noble and _gentle_ youth; _gentle_ blood.

**1.** Mild; meek; soft; bland; not rough, harsh or severe; as a _gentle_ nature, temper or disposition; a _gentle_ manner; a _gentle_ address; a _gentle_ voice. [[1 Thessalonians 2#7|1 Thessalonians 2:7]]. [[1 Thessalonians 2#7|1 Thessalonians 2:7]].

**2.** Tame; peaceable; not wild, turbulent or refractory; as a _gentle_ horse or beast.

**3.** Soothing; pacific.

**4.** Treating with mildness; not violent.

A _gentle_ hand may lead the elephant with a hair.

**GEN'TLE**, _noun_ A gentleman.

**1.** A kind of worm.

**GEN'TLE**, _verb transitive_ To make genteel; to raise from the vulgar.

# Gust

**GUST**, _noun_ \[Latin gustus, gusto; Gr. a contracted word, for it has taste.\]

**1.** Taste; tasting, or the sense of tasting. More generally, the pleasure of tasting; relish.

**2.** Sensual enjoyment.

Where love is duty on the female side,

On theirs, mere sensual _gust_ and sought with surly

pride.

**3.** Pleasure; amusement; gratification.

Destroy all creatures for thy sport or _gust_

**4.** Turn of fancy; intellectual taste.

A choice of it may be made according to the _gust_ and manner of the ancients. \[Taste is now generally used.\]

**GUST**, _verb transitive_ To taste; to have a relish. \[Little used.\]

**GUST**, _noun_

**1.** A sudden squall; a violent blast of wind; a sudden rushing or driving of the wind, of short duration.

**2.** A sudden, violent burst of passion.

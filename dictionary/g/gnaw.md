# Gnaw

**GNAW**, _verb transitive_ naw. \[ Gr. to scrape.\]

**1.** To bite off by little and little; to bite or scrape off with the fore teeth; to wear away by biting. The rats _gnaw_ a board or plank; a worm gnaws the wood of a tree or the plank of a ship.

**2.** To eat by biting off small portions of food with the fore teeth.

**3.** To bite in agony or rage.

They gnawed their tongues for pain. [[Revelation 16#10|Revelation 16:10]].

**4.** To waste; to fret; to corrode.

**5.** To pick with the teeth.

His bones clean picked; his very bones they _gnaw_

**GNAW**, _verb intransitive_ naw. To use the teeth in biting.

I might well, like the spaniel, _gnaw_ upon the chain that ties me.

# Glean

**GLEAN**, _verb transitive_

**1.** To gather the stalks and ears of grain which reapers leave behind them.

Let me now go to the field, and _glean_ ears of corn - [[Ruth 2#2|Ruth 2:2]].

**2.** To collect things thinly scattered; to gather what is left in small parcels or numbers, or what is found in detached parcels; as, to _glean_ a few passages from an author.

They gleaned of them in the highways five thousand men. [[Judges 20#45|Judges 20:45]].

**GLEAN**, _verb intransitive_ To gather stalks or ears of grain left by reapers.

And she went, and came and gleaned in the field after the reapers. [[Ruth 2#3|Ruth 2:3]].

**GLEAN**, _noun_ A collection made by gleaning, or by gathering here and there a little.

The gleans of yellow thyme distend his thighs.

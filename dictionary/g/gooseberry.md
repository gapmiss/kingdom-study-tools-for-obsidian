# Gooseberry

**GOOSEBERRY**, _noun_ goos'berry. \[Latin grossula. The English word is undoubtedly corrupted from crossberry, grossberry, or gorseberry; a name taken from the roughness of the shrub. See [[Cross and Gross]].\]

The fruit of a shrub, and the shrub itself, the Ribes grossularia. The shrub is armed with spines. Of the fruit there are several varieties.

The American _gooseberry_ belongs to the genus Melastoma, and the West Indian, _gooseberry_ to the genus Cactus.

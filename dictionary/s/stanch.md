# Stanch

**STANCH**, _verb transitive_ In a general sense, to stop; to set or fix; but applied only to the blood; to stop the flowing of blood. Cold applications to the neck will often _stanch_ the bleeding of the nose.

**STANCH**, _verb intransitive_ To stop, as blood; to cease to flow.

Immediately the issue of her blood stanched. [[Luke 8#44|Luke 8:44]].

**STANCH**, _adjective_ \[This is the same word as the foregoing, the primary sense of which is to set; hence the sense of firmness.\]

**1.** Sound; firm; strong and tight; as a _stanch_ ship.

**2.** Firm in principle; steady; constant and zealous; hearty; as a _stanch_ churchman; a _stanch_ republican; a _stanch_ friend or adherent.

In politics I hear youre _stanch_

**3.** Strong; not to be broken.

**4.** Firm; close.

This is to be kept _stanch_

A _stanch_ hound, is one that follows the scent closely without error or remissness.

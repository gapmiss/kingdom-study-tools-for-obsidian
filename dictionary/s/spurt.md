# Spurt

**SPURT**, _verb transitive_ \[The English word has suffered a transposition of letters. It is from the root of sprout, which see.\] To throw out, as liquid in a stream; to drive or force out with violence, as a liquid from a pipe or small orifice; as, to _spurt_ water from the mouth, or other liquid from a tube.

**SPURT**, _verb intransitive_ To gush or issue out in a stream, as liquor from a cask; to rush from a confined place in a small stream.

Then the small jet, which hasty hands unlock, spurts in the gardners eyes who turns the cock.

**SPURT**, _noun_

**1.** A sudden or violent ejection or gushing of a liquid substance from a tube, orifice or other confined place; a jet.

**2.** A sudden or short occasion or exigency; sudden effort. \[Vulgar.\]

# Swill

**SWILL**, _verb transitive_

**1.** To drink grossly or greedily; as, to _swill_ down great quantities of liquors.

**2.** To wash; to drench.

**3.** To inebriate; to swell with fullness.

I should be loth

To meet the rudeness and swill'd insolence

Of such late wassailers.

**SWILL**, _noun_ Large draughts of liquor; or drink taken in excessive quantities.

**1.** The wash or mixture of liquid substances, given to swine; called in some places swillings.

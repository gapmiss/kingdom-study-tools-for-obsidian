# Scud

**SCUD**, _verb intransitive_

**1.** In _a gereral sense_, to be driven or to flee or fly with haste. In seamen's language, to be driven with precipitation before a tempest. This is done with a sail extended on the foremast of the ship, or when the wind is too violent, without any sail set, which is called _scudding under_ _bare poles_.

**2.** To run with precipitation; to fly.

**SCUD**, _noun_

**1.** A low thin cloud, or thin clould driven by the wind.

**2.** A driving along; a rushing with precipitation.

# Sir

**SIR**, _noun_ sur.

**1.** A word or respect used in addresses to men, as madam is in addresses to women. It signifies properly lord, corresponding to dominus in Latin, in Spanish, and herr in German. It is used in the singular or plural. Speak on, _sir_ But sirs, be sudden in the execution.

**2.** The title of a knight or baronet; as _sir_ Horace Vere.

**3.** It is used by Shakespeare for man. In the election of a _sir_ so rare. \[Not in use.\]

**4.** In American colleges, the title of a master of arts.

**5.** It is prefixed to loin, in sirloin; as a sirloin of beef. This practice is said to have originated in the knighting of a loin of beef by one of the English kings in a fit of good humor.

**6.** Formerly the title or a priest.

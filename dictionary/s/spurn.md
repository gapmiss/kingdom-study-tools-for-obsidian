# Spurn

**SPURN**, _verb transitive_ \[Latin , spur, kicking.\]

**1.** To kick; to drive back or away, as with the foot.

**2.** To reject with disdain; to scorn to receive or accept. What multitudes of rational beings _spurn_ the offers of eternal happiness!

**3.** To treat with contempt.

**SPURN**, _verb intransitive_

**1.** To manifest disdain in rejecting any thing; as, to _spurn_ at the gracious offers of pardon.

**2.** To make contemptuous opposition; to manifest disdain in resistance.

Nay more, to _spurn_ at your most royal image.

**3.** To kick or toss up the heels.

The drunken chairman in the kennel spurns.

**SPURN**, _noun_ Disdainful rejection; contemptuous treatment.

The insolence of office, and the spurns that patient merit of the unworthy takes.

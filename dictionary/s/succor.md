# Succor

**SUC'COR**, _verb transitive_ \[Latin succurro; sub and curro, to run.\]

Literally, to run to, or run to support; hence, to help or relieve when in difficulty, want or distress; to assist and deliver from suffering; as, to _succor_ a besieged city; to _succor_ prisoners.

He is able to _succor_ them that are tempted. [[Hebrews 2#1|Hebrews 2:1]].

**SUC'COR**, _noun_ Aid; help; assistance; particularly, assistance that relieves and delivers from difficulty, want or distress.

My father

Flying for _succor_ to his servant Banister--

**1.** The person or thing that brings relief.

The city when pressed received succors from an unexpected quarter.

The mighty _succor_ which made glad the foe.

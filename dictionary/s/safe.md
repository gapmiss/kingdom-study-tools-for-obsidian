# Safe

**SAFE**, _adjective_ \[Latin salvus, from salus, safety, health.\]

**1.** Free from danger of any kind; as _safe_ from enemies; _safe_ from disease; _safe_ from storms; _safe_ from the malice of foes.

**2.** Free from hurt, injury or damage; as, to walk _safe_ over red hot plowshares. We brought the goods _safe_ to land.

**3.** Conferring safety; securing from harm; as a _safe_ guide; a _safe_ harbor; a _safe_ bridge.

**4.** Not exposing to danger. [[Philippians 3#1|Philippians 3:1]].

**5.** No longer dangerous; placed beyond the power of doing harm; a ludicrous meaning.

Banquo's _safe_ - Aye, my good lord, _safe_ in a ditch.

**SAFE**, _noun_ A place of safety; a place for securing provisions from noxious animals.

**SAFE**, _verb transitive_ To render _safe_ \[Not in use.\]

# Salient

**SA'LIENT**, _adjective_ \[Latin saliens, salio, to leap.\]

**1.** Leaping; an epithet in heraldry applied to a lion or other beast, represented in a leaping posture, with his right foot in the dexter point, and his hinder left foot in the sinister base of the escutcheon, by which it is distinguished from rampant.

**2.** In fortification, projecting; as a _salient_ angle. A _salient_ angle points outward, and is opposed to a re-entering angle, which points inward.

**SA'LIENT**, _adjective_ \[Latin saliens, from salio, to leap or shoot out.\]

**1.** Leaping; moving by leaps; as frogs.

**2.** Beating; throbbing; as the heart.

**3.** Shooting out or up; springing; darting; as a _salient_ sprout.

# Squabble

**SQUABBLE**, _verb intransitive_ \[I know not the origin of this word, but it seems to be from the root of wabble; G., to vibrate, to quake, to be sleek. See [[Squab]].\]

**1.** To contend for superiority; to scuffle; to struggle; as, two persons _squabble_ in sport.

**2.** To contend; to wrangle; to quarrel.

**3.** To debate peevishly; to dispute. If there must be disputes, it is less criminal to _squabble_ than to murder.

\[Squabble is not an elegant word in any of its uses. In some of them it is low.\]

**SQUABBLE**, _noun_ A scuffle; a wrangle; a brawl; a petty quarrel.

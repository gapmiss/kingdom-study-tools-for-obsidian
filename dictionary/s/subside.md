# Subside

**SUBSI'DE**, _verb intransitive_ \[Latin subsido; sub and sido, to settle. See [[Set]].\]

**1.** To sink or fall to the bottom; to settle; as lees.

**2.** To fall into a state of quiet; to cease to rage; to be calmed; to become tranquil. Let the passions _subside_ The tumults of war will _subside_ Christ commanded, and the storm subsided.

**3.** To tend downwards; to sink; as a subsiding hill. The land subsides into a plain.

**4.** To abate; to be reduced.

In cases of danger, pride and envy naturally _subside_

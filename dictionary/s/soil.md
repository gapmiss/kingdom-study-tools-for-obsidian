# Soil

**SOIL**, _verb transitive_

**1.** To make dirty on the surface; to foul; to dirt; to stain; to defile; to tarnish; to sull; as, to _soil_ a garment with dust. Out wonted ornaments now soil'd and stain'd.

**2.** To cover or tinge with any thing extraneous; as, to _soil_ the earth with blood.

**3.** To dung; to manure.

**TO _soil_ A HORSE**, is to purge him by giving him fresh grass.

**TO _soil_ A CATTLE**, in husbandry, is to feed them with grass daily mowed for them, instead of pasturing them.

**SOIL**, _noun_

**1.** Dirt; and foul matter upon another substance; foulness; apot.

**2.** Stain; tarnish. A lady's honor will not bear a _soil_

**3.** The upper stratum of the earth; the mold, or that compound substance with furnishes nutriment to plants, or which is particulary adapted to support and nourish them.

**4.** Land; country. We love our native _soil_

**5.** Dung; compost. Improve land by dung and other sort of soils.

**TO TAKE SOIL**, to run into the water, as a deer when pursued.

# Seclude

**SECLU'DE**, _verb transitive_ \[**L**. _secludo; se_ and _claudo, cludo,_ to shut.\]

**1.** To separate, as from company or society, and usually to keep apart for some length of tome, or to confine in a separate state; as persons in low spirits _seclude_ themselves from society.

Let eastern tyrants from the light of heav'n _Seclude_ their bosom slaves. _Thomson._

**2.** To shut out; to prevent from entering; to preclude.

Inclose your tender plants in your conservatory, _secluding_ all entrance of cold.

_Evelyn._

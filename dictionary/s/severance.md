# Severance

**SEV'ERANCE**, _noun_ Separation; the act of dividing or disuniting. The _sevrance_ of a jointure is make by destroying the unity of interest. Thus when there are two joint-tenants for life, and the inheritance is purchased by or descends upon either, it is a _servrance_.

So also when two persons are joined in a writ, and one is nonsuited; in this case _sevrance_ is permitted, and the other plantif may proceed in the suit. So also in assize, when two or more disseizees appear upon the writ, and not the other, _sevrance_ is permitted.

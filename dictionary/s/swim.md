# Swim

**SWIM**, _verb intransitive_

**1.** To float; to be supported on water or other fluid; not to sink. Most species of wood will _swim_ in water. Any substance will _swim_ whose specific gravity is less than that of the fluid in which it is immersed.

**2.** To move progressively in water by means of the motion of the hands and feet, or of fins. In Paris, boys are taught to _swim_ by instructors appointed for that purpose. [[Isaiah 25#11|Isaiah 25:11]].

Leap in with me into this angry flood,

And _swim_ to yonder point.

**3.** To float; to be borne along by a current. In all states there are men who will _swim_ with the tide of popular opinion.

**4.** To glide along with a smooth motion, or with a waving motion.

She with pretty and with swimming gait.

A hov'ring mist came swimming o'er his sight.

**5.** To be dizzy or vertiginous; to have a waving motion of the head or a sensation of that kind, or a reeling of the body. The head swims when we walk on high.

**6.** To be floated; to be overflowed or drenched; as, the earth swims in rain.

Sudden the ditches swell, the meadows _swim_

All the night I make my bed to swim; I water my couch with my tears. [[Psalms 6#6|Psalms 6:6]].

**7.** To overflow; to abound; to have abundance.

They now _swim_ in joy.

**SWIM**, _verb transitive_ To pass or move on; as, to _swim_ a stream. Deer are known to _swim_ rivers and sounds.

Sometimes he thought to _swim_ the stormy main.

**1.** To immerse in water that the lighter parts may swim; as, to _swim_ wheat for seed.

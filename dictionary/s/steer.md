# Steer

**STEER**, _noun_ A young male of the ox kind or common ox. It is rendered in Dutch, a bull; but in the United States, this name is generally given to a castrated male of the ox kind, from two to four years old.

With solemn pomp then sacrificd a _steer_

**1.** To direct; to govern; particularly, to direct and govern the course of a ship by the movements of the helm. Hence,

**2.** To direct; to guide; to show the way or course to.

That with a staff his feeble steps did _steer_

**STEER**, _verb intransitive_

**1.** To direct and govern a ship or other vessel in its course. Formerly seamen steered by the stars; they ow _steer_ by the compass.

A ship--where the wind veers oft, as oft so steers and shifts her sail.

**2.** To be directed and governed; as, a ship steers with ease.

**3.** To conduct ones self; to take or pursue a course or way.

**STEER**, _noun_ A rudder or helm. \[Not in use.\]

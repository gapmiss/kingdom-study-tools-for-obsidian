# Scold

**SCOLD**, _verb intransitive_

To find fault or rail with rude clamor; to brawl; to utter railing or harsh, rude, boisterous rebuke; with at; as, to _scold_ at a servant. A scolding tongue, a scolding wife, a scolding husband, a scolding master, who can endure?

Pardon me, 'tis the first time that ever I'm forc'd to _scold_

**SCOLD**, _verb transitive_ To chide with rudeness and boisterous clamor; to rate. \[The transitive use of this word is of recent origin, at least within my knowledge.\]

**SCOLD**, _noun_

**1.** A rude, clamorous, foul-mouthed woman.

**SCOLD**s answer foul-mouth'd scolds.

**2.** A scolding; a brawl.

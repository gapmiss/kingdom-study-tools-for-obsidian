# Scrutiny

**SCRU'TINY**, _noun_ \[Latin _scrutinium_, from s_crutor_, to search closely, to pry into.\]

**1.** Close search; minute inquiry; critical examinatiion; as a _scrutiny_ of votes; narrower

_scrutiny_. In the heat of debate, observations may escape a prudent man which will not bear the test of _scrutiny_.

**2.** In _the primitive church_, an examination of catechumens in the last week of Lent, who were to receive baptism on Easter-day. This was performed with prayers, exorcisms and many other ceremonies.

**3.** In _the canon law_, a ticket or little paper billet on which a vote is written.

# Swoop

**SWOOP**, _verb transitive_ \[This is probably from sweep, or the same root.\]

**1.** To fall on at once and seize; to catch while on the wing; as, a hawk swoops a chicken; a kite swoops up a mouse.

**2.** To seize; to catchup; to take with a sweep.

**3.** To pass with violence.

**SWOOP**, _verb intransitive_ To pass with pomp.

**SWOOP**, _noun_ A falling on and seizing, as of a rapacious fowl on his prey.

The eagle fell--and carried away a whole litter of cubs at a _swoop_

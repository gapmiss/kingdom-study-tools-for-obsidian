# Sunder

**SUN'DER**, _verb transitive_

**1.** To part; to separate; to divide; to disunite in almost any manner, either by rending, cutting, or breaking; as, to _sunder_ a rope or cord; to _sunder_ a limb or joint; to _sunder_ friends, or the ties of friendship. The executioner sunders the head from the body at a stroke. A mountain may be sundered by an earthquake.

Bring me lightning, give me thunder;

\--Jove may kill, but ne'er shall _sunder_

**2.** To expose to the sun. \[Provincial in England.\]

**SUN'DER**, _noun_ In _sunder_ in tow.

He cutteth the spear in _sunder_ [[Psalms 46#9|Psalms 46:9]].

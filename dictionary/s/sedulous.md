# Sedulous

**SED'ULOUS**, _adjective_. \[Latin _sedulus_, from the root of _sedeo_, to sit; as _assiduous_ from _assideo_.\] Literally, sitting close to an employment; hence, assiduous; diligent in application or pursuit; constant, steady and persevering in business or endeavors to effect an object; steadily industrious; as the _sedulous_ bee.

What signifies the sound of words in prayer, without the affection of heart, and a _sedulous_ application of the proper means that may lead to such an end? _L'Estrange_.

# Symbol

**SYM'BOL**, _noun_ \[Latin symbolum; Gr. with, and to throw; to compare.\]

**1.** The sign or representation of any moral thing by the images or properties of natural things. Thus the lion is the _symbol_ of courage; the lamb is the _symbol_ of meekness or patience. Symbols are of various kinds, as types, enigmas, parables, fables, allegories, emblems, hieroglyphics, etc.

**2.** An emblem or representation of something else. Thus in the eucharist, the bread and wine are called symbols of the body and blood of Christ.

**3.** A letter or character which is significant. The Chinese letters are most of them symbols. The symbols in algebra are arbitrary.

**4.** In medals, a certain mark or figure representing a being or thing, as a trident is the _symbol_ of Neptune, the peacock of June, etc.

**5.** Among christians, an abstract or compendium; the creed, or a summary of the articles of religion.

**6.** Lot; sentence of adjudication. \[Not in use.\]

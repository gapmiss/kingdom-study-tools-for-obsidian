# Sociable

**SO'CIABLE**, _adjective_ \[Latin sociabilis, from socius, a companion, probably from sequor, to follow. See [[Seek]].\]

**1.** That may be conjoined; fit to be united in one body or company; as _sociable_ parts united in one body.

**2.** Ready or disposed to unite in a general interest. To make man mild, and _sociable_ to man.

**3.** Ready and inclined to join in company or society; or frequently meeting for conversation; as _sociable_ neighbors.

**4.** Inclined to converse when in company; disposed to freedom in conversation; opposed to reserved and taciturn.

**5.** Free in conversation; conversing much or familiarly. The guests were very _sociable_

# Sue

**SUE**, _verb transitive_ su. \[Latin sequor. See [[Seek and Essay]].\]

**1.** To seek justice or right from one by legal process; to institute process in law against one; to prosecute in a civil action for the recovery of a real or supposed right; as, to _sue_ one for debt; to _sue_ one for damages in trespass. [[Matthew 5#40|Matthew 5:40]].

**2.** To gain by legal process.

**3.** To clean the beak, as a hawk; a term of falconry.

To _sue_ out, to petition for and take out; or to apply for and obtain; as, to _sue_ out a writ in chancery; to _sue_ out a pardon for a criminal.

**SUE**, _verb intransitive_ To prosecute; to make legal claim; to seek for in law; as, to _sue_ for damages.

**1.** To seek by request; to apply for; to petition; to entreat.

By adverse destiny constrain'd to sue

For counsel and redress, he sues to you.

**2.** To make interest for; to demand.

Caesar come to Rome to _sue_ for the double honor of a triumph and the consulship.

# Struggle

**STRUGGLE**, _verb intransitive_ \[This word may be formed on the root of stretch, right, etc. which signifies to strain; or more directly on the same elements in Latin , to wrinkle.\]

**1.** Properly, to strive, or to make efforts with a twisting or with contortions of the body. Hence,

**2.** To use great efforts; to labor hard; to strive; to contend; as, to _struggle_ to save life; to _struggle_ with the waves; to _struggle_ against the stream; to _struggle_ with adversity.

**3.** To labor in pain or anguish; to be in agony; to labor in any kind of difficulty or distress.

Tis wisdom to beware, and better shun the bait than _struggle_ in the snare.

**STRUGGLE**, _noun_

**1.** Great labor; forcible effort to obtain an object, or to avoid an evil; properly, a violent effort with contortions of the body.

**2.** Contest; contention; strife.

An honest man might look upon the _struggle_ with indifference.

**3.** Agony; contortions of extreme distress.

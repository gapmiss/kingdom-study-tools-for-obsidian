# Snarl

**SN'ARL**, _verb intransitive_ \[This word seems to be allied to gnarl, and to proceed from some root signifyingto twist, bind, or fasten, or to involve, entangle, and thus to be allied to snare.\]

**1.** To growl, as an angry or surly dog; to gnarl; to utter grumbling sounds; but it expresses more violence than grumble. That I should _snarl_ and bit and play the dog.

**2.** To speak roughly; to talk in rude murmuring terms. It is malicious and unmanly to _snarl_ at the little lapses of a pen, from with Virgil himself stands not exempted.

**SN'ARL**, _verb transitive_

**1.** To entangle; to complicate; to involve in knots; as, to _snarl_ the hair; to _snarl_ a skain of thread. \[This word is in universal popular use in New England.\]

**2.** To embarrass.

**SN'ARL**, _noun_ Entanglement; a knot or complication of hair, thread, etc., which it is difficult to disentangle.

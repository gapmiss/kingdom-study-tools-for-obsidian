# Smooth

**SMOOTH**, _adjective_ \[Latin mitis.\]

**1.** Having an even surface, or a surface so even that no roughness or points are perceptible to the touch; not rough; as _smooth_ glass; _smooth_ porcelain. The out lines must be _smooth_ imperceptible to the touch.

**2.** To free from obstruction; to make easy. Thou, Abelard, the last sad office pay, And _smooth_ my passage to the realms of day.

**3.** To free from harshness; to make flowing. In their motions harmony divine so smooths her charming tones.'

**4.** To palliate; to soften; as, to _smooth_ a fault.

**5.** To calm; to mollify; to allay. Each perturbation smooth'd with outward calm.

**6.** To ease. The difficulty smoothed.

**7.** To flatter; to soften with blandishments. Because I cannot flatter and look fair, smile in men's faces, _smooth_ deceive and coy.

# Seem

**SEEM**, _v_. _i_.

**1.** To appear; to make or have a show or semblance.

Thou art not what thou _seems't_. _Shak_.

All _seem'd_ well pleased; all _seem'd_, but were not all. _Milton_.

**2.** To have the appearance of truth or fact; to be understood as true. It _seems_ that the Turkish power is on the decline.

A prince of Italy, it _seems_, entertained his mistress on a great lake. _Addison_.

**SEEM**, _v_. _t_. To become; to befit. _Obs_.

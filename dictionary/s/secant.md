# Secant

**SE'CANT**, _adjective_ \[**L**. _secans, seco,_ to cut or cut off, coinciding with Eng. _saw_.\] Cutting; dividing into two parts.

**SE'CANT**, _noun_

**1.** In _geometry_, a line that cuts another, or divides it into parts. The _secant_ of a circle is a line drawn from the circumference on one side, to a point without the circumference on the other, In _trigonometry_, a _secant_ is a right line drawn from the center of a circle, which, cutting the circumference, proceeds till it meets with a tangent to the same circle.

**2.** In _trigonometry_, the _secant_ of an arc is a right line drawn from the center through one end of the arc, and terminated by a tangent drawn through the other end.

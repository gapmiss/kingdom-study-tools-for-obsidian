# Scuttle

**SCUT'TLE**, _noun_ \[**L**. _scutella_, a pan or saucer.\] A broad shallow basket; so called from its resemblance to a dish.

**SCUT'TLE**, _noun_

**1.** In ships, a small hatchway or opening in the deck, large enough to admit a man, and with a lid for covering it; also, a like hole in the side of a ship, and through the coverings of her hatchways, etc.

**2.** A square hole in the roof of a house, with a lid.

**3.** \[from _scud_, and properly _scuddle_.\] A quick pace; a short run.

**SCUT'TLE**, _verb intransitive_ To run with affected precipitation.

**SCUT'TLE**, _verb transitive_ \[from the noun.\]

**1.** To cut large holes through the bottom or sides of a ship for any purpose.

**2.** To sink by making holes through the bottom; as, to _scuttle_ a ship.

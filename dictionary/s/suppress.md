# Suppress

**SUPPRESS**', _verb transitive_ \[Latin suppressus, supprimo; sub and premo, to press.\]

**1.** To overpower and crush; to subdue; to destroy; as, to _suppress_ a rebellion; to _suppress_ a mutiny or riot; to _suppress_ opposition.

Every rebellion when it is suppressed, makes the subject weaker, and the government stronger.

**2.** To keep in; to restrain from utterance or vent; as, to _suppress_ the voice; to _suppress_ sighs.

**3.** To retain without disclosure; to conceal; not to tell or reveal; as, to _suppress_ evidence.

She suppresses the name, and this keeps him in a pleasing suspense.

**4.** To retain without communication or making public; as, to _suppress_ a letter; to _suppress_ a manuscript.

**5.** To stifle; to stop; to hinder from circulation; as, to _suppress_ a report.

**6.** To stop; to restrain; to obstruct from discharges; as, to _suppress_ a diarrhea, a hemorrhage and the like.

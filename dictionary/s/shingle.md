# Shingle

**SHIN'GLE**, _noun_ \[Gr.; Latin _scinkula_, from _scindo_.\]

**1.** A thin board sawed or rived for covering buildings. Shingles are of different lengths, with one end much thinner than the other for lapping. They are used for covering roofs and sometimes the body of the builking.

**2.** Round gravel, or a collection of roundish stones.

The plain of La Crau in France, is composed of _shingle_. _Pinkerton_.

**3.** _Shingles_, _plural_ \[Latin _cingulum_, \] a kind of tetter or herpes which spreads around the body like a girdle; an eruptive disease.

**SHIN'GLE**, _verb transitive_ To cover with shingles; as, to _shingle_ a roof.

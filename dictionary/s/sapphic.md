# Sapphic

**SAPPHIC**, _adjective_ saf'ic. Pertaining to Sappho, a Grecian poetess; as _sapphic_ odes; _sapphic_ verse. The _sapphic_ verse consists of eleven syllables in five feet, of which the first, fourth and fifth are trochees, the second a spondee, and the third a dactyl, in the first three lines of each stanza, with a fourth consisting only of a dactyl and a spondee.

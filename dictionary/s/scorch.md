# Scorch

**SCORCH**, _verb transitive_

**1.** To burn superficially; to subject to a degree of heat that changes the color of a thing, or both the color and texture of the surface. Fire will _scorch_ linen or cotton very speedily in extremely cold weather.

**2.** To burn; to affect painfully with heat. Scorched with the burning sun or burning sands of Africa.

**SCORCH**, _verb intransitive_ To be burnt on the surface; to be parched; to be dried up.

Scatter a little mungy straw and fern among your seedlings, to prevent the roots from scorching.

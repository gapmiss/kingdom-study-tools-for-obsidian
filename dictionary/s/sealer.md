# Sealer

**SE'ALER**, _noun_

**1.** One who seals; an officer in chancery who seals writs and instruments.

**2.** In _New England_, an officer appointed by the town or other proper authority, to examine and try weithts and measures, and set a stamp on such as are according to the standards established by the state; also, an officer who inspects lether and stamps such as is good. These are called _sealers_ of weights and measures, and _sealers_ of _lethers_.

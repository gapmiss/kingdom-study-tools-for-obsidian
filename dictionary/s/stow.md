# Stow

**STOW**, _verb transitive_ \[G. Latin , to crowd, to stuff; the handle of a plow. The sense is to set or throw down, from the more general sense of throwing, driving.\]

**1.** To place; to put in a suitable place or position; as, to _stow_ bags, bales or casks in a ships hold; to _stow_ hay in a mow; to _stow_ sheaves. The word has reference to the placing of many thing, or of one thing among many, or of a mass of things.

**2.** To lay up; to reposit.

**STOW** in names, signifies place, as in Barstow.

# Prowl

**PROWL**, _verb transitive_ \[I know not the origin of this word, nor from what source it is derived. It may be derived from the root of stroll, troll, with a different prefix.\] To rove over.

He prowls each place, still in new colors deck'd.

**PROWL**, _verb intransitive_ To rove or wander, particularly for prey, as a wild

beast; as a prowling wolf.

**1.** To rove and plunder; to prey; to plunder.

**PROWL**, _noun_ A roving for prey; colloquially, something to be seized and devoured.

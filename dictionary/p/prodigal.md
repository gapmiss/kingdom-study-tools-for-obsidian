# Prodigal

**PROD'IGAL**, _adjective_ \[Latin produgus, from prodigo, to drive forth, to lavish.\]

**1.** Given to extravagant expenditures; expending money or other things without necessity; profuse, lavish; wasteful; not frugal or economical; as a _prodigal_ man; the _prodigal_ son. A man may be _prodigal_ of his strength, of his health, of his life or blood, as well as of his money.

**2.** Profuse, lavish; expended to excess or without necessity; as _prodigal_ expenses.

**3.** Very liberal; profuse. Nature is _prodigal_ of her bounties.

**PROD'IGAL**, _noun_ One that expends money extravagantly or without necessity; one that is profuse or lavish; a waster; a spendthrift.

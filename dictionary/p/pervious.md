# Pervious

**PER'VIOUS**, _adjective_ \[Latin pervius; per and via, way, or from the root of that word.\]

**1.** Admitting passage; that may be penetrated by another body or substance; permeable; penetrable. We say, glass is _pervious_ to light; a porous stone is _pervious_ to water; a wood is _pervious_ or not _pervious_ to a body of troops.

A country _pervious_ to the arms and authority of the conqueror.

**2.** That may be penetrated by the mental sight.

By darkness they mean God, whose secrets are _pervious_ to no eye.

**3.** Pervading; permeating; as _pervious_ fire. \[Not proper.\]

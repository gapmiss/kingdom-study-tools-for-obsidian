# Preponderate

**PREPOND'ERATE**, _verb transitive_ \[Latin proepondero; proe, before, and pondero, to weigh.\]

**1.** To outweigh; to overpower by weight.

An inconsiderable weight, by distance from the center of the balance, will _preponderate_ greater magnitudes.

**2.** To overpower by stronger influence or moral power.

**PREPOND'ERATE**, _verb intransitive_ To exceed in weight; hence, to incline or descend, as the scale of a balance.

That is no just balance in which the heaviest side will not _preponderate_

**1.** To exceed in influence or power; hence, to incline to one side.

By putting every argument on one side and the other, into the balance, we must form a judgment which side preponderates.

# Peck

**PECK**, _noun_

**1.** The fourth part of a bushel; a dry measure of eight quarts; as a _peck_ of wheat or oats.

**2.** In low language, a great deal; as, to be in a _peck_ of troubles.

**PECK**, _verb transitive_

**1.** To strike with the beak; to thrust the beak into, as a bird that pecks a hole in a tree.

**2.** To strike with a pointed instrument, or to delve or dig with any thing pointed, as with a pick-ax.

**3.** To pick up food with the beak.

**4.** To strike with small and repeated blows; to strike in manner to make small impressions. In this sense, the verb is generally intransitive. We say, to _peck_ at.\]

\[This verb and pick are radically the same.\]

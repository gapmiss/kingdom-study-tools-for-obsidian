# Per

**PER**, a Latin preposition, denoting through, passing, or over the whole extent, as in perambulo. Hence it is sometimes equivalent to very in English, as in peracutus, very sharp. As a prefix, in English, it retains these significations, and in chemistry it is used to denote very of fully, to the utmost extent, as in peroxyd, a substance oxydated to the utmost degree.

**PER** is used also for by, as _per_ bearer, by the bearer.

**PER** annum, \[Latin\] by the year; in each year successively.

**PER** se, \[Latin\] by himself; by itself; by themselves.

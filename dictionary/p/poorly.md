# Poorly

**POOR'LY**, _adverb_ Without wealth; in indigence or want of the conveniences and comforts of life; as, to live _poorly_

**1.** With little or no success; with little growth, profit or advantage; as, wheat grows _poorly_ on the Atlantic borders of New England; these men have succeeded _poorly_ in business.

**2.** Meanly; without spirit.

Nor is their courage or their wealth so low,

That from his wars they _poorly_ would retire.

**3.** Without excellence or dignity. He performs _poorly_ in elevated characters.

**POOR'LY**, _adjective_ Somewhat ill; indisposed; not in health; a common use of the word in America.

For three or four weeks past I have lost ground, having been _poorly_ in health.

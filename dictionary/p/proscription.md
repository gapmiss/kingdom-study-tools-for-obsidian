# Proscription

**PROSCRIP'TION**, _noun_ \[Latin proscriptio.\] The act of proscribing or dooming to death; among the Romans, the public offer of a reward for the head of a political enemy. Such were the proscriptions of Sylla and Marius. Under the triumvirate, many of the best Roman citizens fell by _proscription_

**1.** A putting out of the protection of law; condemning to exile.

**2.** Censure and condemnation; utter rejection.

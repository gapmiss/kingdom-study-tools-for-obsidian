# Patrician

**PATRI'CIAN**, _adjective_ \[Latin patricius, from pater, father.\] Senatorial; noble; not plebeian. This epithet is derived from the Roman patres, fathers, the title of Roman senators; as _patrician_ birth or blood; _patrician_ families.

**PATRI'CIAN**, _noun_ A nobleman. In the Roman state, the patricians were the descendants of the first Roman senators.

# Profuse

**PROFU'SE**, _adjective_ \[Latin profusus, profundo, to pour out; pro and fundo.\]

**1.** Lavish; liberal to excess; prodigal; as a _profuse_ government; a _profuse_ administration. Henry the eighth, a _profuse_ king, dissipated the treasures which the parsimony of his father had amassed. A man's friends are generally too _profuse_ of praise, and his enemies too sparing.

**2.** Extravagant; lavish; as _profuse_ expenditures.

**3.** Overabounding; exuberant.

On a green shady bank, _profuse_ of flowers--

O liberty! thou goddess heavenly bright,

Profuse of bliss--

Profuse ornament in painting, architecture or gardening, as well as in dress or in language, shows a mean or corrupted taste.

**PROFU'SE**, _verb transitive_ s as z. To pour out. \[Little used.\]

**1.** To squander. \[Little used.\]

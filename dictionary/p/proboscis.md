# Proboscis

**PROBOS'CIS**, _noun_ \[Latin from Gr. before, and to feed or graze.\]

The snout or trunk of an elephant and of other animals, particularly of insects. The _proboscis_ of an elephant is a flexible muscular pipe or canal of about 8 feet in length, and is properly the extension of the nose. This is the instrument with which he takes food and carries it to his mouth. The _proboscis_ of insects is used to suck blood from animals, or juice from plants.

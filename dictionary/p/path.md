# Path

**P'ATH**, _noun_ _plural_ paths. \[Gr. to tread.\]

**1.** A way beaten or trodden by the feet of man or beast, or made hard by wheels; that part of a highway on which animals or carriages ordinarily pass; applied to the ground only, and never to a paved street in a city.

**2.** Any narrow way beaten by the foot.

**3.** The way, course or track where a body moves in the atmosphere or in space; as the _path_ of a planet or comet; the _path_ of a meteor.

**4.** A way or passage.

**5.** Course of life.

He marketh all my paths. [[Job 33#11|Job 33:11]].

**6.** Precepts; rules prescribed.

Uphold my going in thy paths. [[Psalms 17#4|Psalms 17:4]].

**7.** Course of providential dealings; moral government.

All the paths of the Lord are mercy and truth to such as keep his covenant. [[Psalms 25#4|Psalms 25:4]].

**P'ATH**, _verb transitive_ To make a _path_ by treading; to beat a _path_ as in snow.

To push forward; to cause to go; to make way for.

**P'ATH**, _verb intransitive_ To walk abroad.

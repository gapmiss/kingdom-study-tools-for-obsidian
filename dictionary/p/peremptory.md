# Peremptory

**PER'EMPTORY**, _adjective_ \[Latin peremptorius, from peremptus, taken away, killed.\]

**1.** Express; positive; absolute; decisive; authoritative; in a manner to preclude debate or expostulation. The orders of the commander are _peremptory_

**2.** Positive in opinion or judgment. The genuine effect of sound learning is to make men less _peremptory_ in their determinations.

**3.** Final; determinate.

**4.** _peremptory_ challenge, in law, a challenge or right of challenging jurors without showing cause.

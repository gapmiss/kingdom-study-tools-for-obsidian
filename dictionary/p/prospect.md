# Prospect

**PROS'PECT**, _noun_ \[Latin prospectus, prospicio, to look forward; pro and specio, to see.\]

**1.** View of things within the reach of the eye.

Eden and all the coast in _prospect_ lay.

**2.** View of things to come; intellectual sight; expectation. The good man enjoys the _prospect_ of future felicity.

**3.** That which is presented to the eye; the place and the objects seen. There is a noble _prospect_ from the dome of the state house in Boston, a _prospect_ diversified with land and water, and every thing that can please the eye.

**4.** Object of view.

Man to himself

Is a large _prospect_

**5.** View delineated or painted; picturesque representation of a landscape.

**6.** Place which affords an extended view.

**7.** Position of the front of a building; as a _prospect_ towards the south or north. [[Ezekiel 40#44|Ezekiel 40:44]].

**8.** Expectation, or ground of expectation. There is a _prospect_ of a good harvest. A man has a _prospect_ of preferment; or he has little _prospect_ of success.

**9.** A looking forward; a regard to something future.

Is he a prudent man as to his temporal estate, who lays designs only for a day, without any _prospect_ to or provision for the remaining part of life? \[Little used.\]

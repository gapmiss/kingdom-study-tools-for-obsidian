# Pollock

**POL'LOCK**

POLLU'TE, _verb transitive_ \[Latin polluo; polluceo and possideo.\]

**1.** To defile; to make foul or unclean; in a general sense. But appropriately, among the Jews, to make unclean or impure, in a legal or ceremonial sense, so as to disqualify a person for sacred services, or to render things unfit for sacred uses. [[Numbers 18#1|Numbers 18:1]]. [[Exodus 20#1|Exodus 20:1]]. [[2 Kings 23#1|2 Kings 23:1]]. [[2 Chronicles 36#1|2 Chronicles 36:1]].

**2.** To taint with guilt.

Ye pollute yourselves with all your idols. [[Ezekiel 20#1|Ezekiel 20:1]].

**3.** To profane; to use for carnal or idolatrous purposes.

My sabbaths they greatly polluted. [[Ezekiel 20#1|Ezekiel 20:1]].

**4.** To corrupt or impair by mixture of ill, moral or physical.

Envy you my praise, and would destroy

With grief my pleasures, and pollute my joy?

**5.** To violate by illegal sexual commerce.

**POLLU'TE**, _adjective_ Polluted; defiled.

# Prescribe

**PRESCRI'BE**, _verb transitive_ \[Latin proescribo, to write before.\]

**1.** In medicine, to direct, as a remedy to be used or applied to a diseased patient. Be not offended with the physician who prescribes harsh remedies.

**2.** To set or lay down authoritatively for direction; to give as a rule of conduct; as, to _prescribe_ laws or rules.

There's joy, when to wild will you laws _prescribe_

**3.** To direct.

Let streams _prescribe_ their fountains where to run.

**PRESCRI'BE**, _verb intransitive_ To write or give medical directions; to direct what remedies are to be used; as, to _prescribe_ for a patient in a fever.

**1.** To give law; to influence arbitrarily.

A forwardness to _prescribe_ to the opinions of others.

**2.** In law, to claim by prescription; to claim a title to a thing by immemorial use and enjoyment; with for. A man may be allowed to _prescribe_ for a right of way, a common or the like; a man cannot _prescribe_ for a castle; he can _prescribe_ only for incorporeal hereditaments.

**3.** To influence by long use.

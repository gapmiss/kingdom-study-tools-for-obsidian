# Purse

**PURSE**, _noun_ purs. \[Latin byrsa, an ox hide; Gr. id.\]

**1.** A small bag in which money is contained or carried in the pocket. It was formerly made of leather, and is still made of this material by common people. It is usually of silk net-work.

**2.** A sum of money offered as the prize of winning in a horse race.

**3.** In turkey, a sum of money, about f50 sterling, or $222.

**4.** The public coffers; the treasury; as, to exhaust a nation's _purse_ or the public _purse_

Long _purse_ or heavy _purse_ wealth; riches.

Light _purse_ or empty _purse_ poverty, or want of resources.

Sword and _purse_ the military power and wealth of a nation.

**PURSE**, _verb transitive_ To put in a _purse_

**1.** To contract into folds or wrinkles.

Thou didst contract and _purse_ thy blow.

# Purge

**PURGE**, _verb transitive_ purj. \[Latin purgo.\]

**1.** To cleanse or purify by separating and carrying off whatever is impure, heterogeneous, foreign or superfluous; as, to _purge_ the body by evacuation; to _purge_ the Augean stable. It is followed by away, of, or off. We say, to _purge_ away or to _purge_ off filth, and to _purge_ a liquor of its scum.

**2.** To clear from guilt or moral defilement; as, to _purge_ one of guilt or crime; to _purge_ away sin.

**PURGE** away our sins, for thy name's sake. [[Psalms 79#9|Psalms 79:9]].

**PURGE** me with hyssop and I shall be clean. [[Psalms 51#7|Psalms 51:7]].

**3.** To clear from accusation or the charge of a crime, as in ordeal.

**4.** To remove what is offensive; to sweep away impurities. [[Ezekiel 20#38|Ezekiel 20:38]].

**5.** To clarify; to defecate; as liquors.

**PURGE**, _verb intransitive_ To become pure by clarification.

**1.** To have frequent or preternatural evacuations by stool.

**PURGE**, _noun_ A medicine that evacuates the body by stool; a cathartic.

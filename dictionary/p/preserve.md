# Preserve

**PRESERVE**, _verb transitive_ prezerv'. \[Low Latin proeservo; proe and servo, to keep.\]

**1.** To keep or save from injury or destruction; to defend from evil.

God did send me before you to _preserve_ life. [[Genesis 45#5|Genesis 45:5]].

O Lord, _preserve_ me from the violent man. [[Psalms 140#1|Psalms 140:1]].

**2.** To uphold; to sustain.

O Lord, thou preservest man and beast. [[Psalms 36#6|Psalms 36:6]].

**3.** To save from decay; to keep in a sound state; as, to _preserve_ fruit in winter. Salt is used to _preserve_ meat.

**4.** To season with sugar or other substances for preservation; as, to _preserve_ plums, quinces or other fruit.

**5.** To keep or defend from corruption; as, to _preserve_ youth from vice.

**PRESERVE**, _noun_ preserv'. Fruit or a vegetable seasoned and kept in sugar or sirup.

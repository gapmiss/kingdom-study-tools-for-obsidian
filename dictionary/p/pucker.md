# Pucker

**PUCK'ER**, _verb transitive_ \[Latin pectus; Gr. signifies closely, densely, to cover.\] To gather into small folds or wrinkles; to contract into ridges and furrows; to corrugate.

His face pale and withered, and his skin puckered in wrinkles.

It is usually followed by up; as, to _pucker_ up cloth; but up is superfluous. It is a popular word, but not elegant.

**PUCK'ER**, _noun_ A fold or wrinkle, or a collection of folds.

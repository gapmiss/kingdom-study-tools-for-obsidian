# Proxy

**PROX'Y**, _noun_ \[contracted from procuracy, or some word from the root of procure, proctor.\]

**1.** The agency of another who acts as a substitute for his principal; agency of a substitute; appearance of a representative. None can be familiar by _proxy_ None can be virtuous or wise by _proxy_

**2.** The person who is substituted or deputed to act for another. A wise man will not commit important business to a _proxy_ when he can transact it in person. In England, any peer may make another lord of parliament his _proxy_ to vote for him in his absence.

**3.** In popular use, an election or day of voting for officers of government.

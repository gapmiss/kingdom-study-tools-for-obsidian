# Proffer

**PROF'FER**, _verb transitive_ \[Latin profero; pro and fero, to bear.\]

**1.** To offer for acceptance; as, to _proffer_ a gift; to _proffer_ services; to _proffer_ friendship.

**2.** To essay or attempt of one's own accord.

So hardy as to _proffer_ or accept

Alone the dreadful voyage.

**PROF'FER**, _noun_ An offer made; something proposed for acceptance by another; as proffers of peace or friendship.

He made a _proffer_ to lay down his commission of command in the army.

**1.** Essay; attempt.

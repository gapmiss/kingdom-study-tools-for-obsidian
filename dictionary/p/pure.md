# Pure

**PURE**, _adjective_ \[Latin purus.\]

**1.** Separate from all heterogeneous or extraneous matter; clear; free from mixture; as _pure_ water; _pure_ clay; _pure_ sand; _pure_ air; _pure_ silver of gold. _pure_ wine is very scare.

**2.** Free from moral defilement; without spot; not sullied or tarnished; incorrupt; undebased by moral turpitude; holy.

Thou art of purer eyes than to behold evil. [[Habakkuk 1#13|Habakkuk 1:13]]. [[Proverbs 20#9|Proverbs 20:9]].

**3.** Genuine; real; true; incorrupt; unadulterated; as _pure_ religion. [[James 1#27|James 1:27]].

**4.** Unmixed; separate from any other subject or from every thing foreign; as _pure_ mathematics.

**5.** Free from guilt; guiltless; innocent.

No hand of strife is _pure_ but that which wins.

**6.** Not vitiated with improper or corrupt words or phrases; as a _pure_ style of discourse or composition.

**7.** Disinterested; as _pure_ benevolence.

**8.** Chaste; as a _pure_ virgin.

**9.** Free from vice or moral turpitude. [[Titus 1#15|Titus 1:15]].

**10.** Ceremonially clean; unpolluted. [[Ezra 6#20|Ezra 6:20]].

**11.** Free from any thing improper; as, his motives are _pure_

**12.** Mere; absolute; that and that only; unconnected with any thing else; as a _pure_ villain. He did that from _pure_ compassion, or _pure_ good nature.

**PURE**, _verb transitive_ To purify; to cleanse. \[Not in use.\]

# Perplex

**PERPLEX**', _verb transitive_ \[Latin perplexus, perplexor; per and plector, to twist; Latin plico, to fold.\]

**1.** To make intricate; to involve; to entangle; to make complicated and difficult to be understood or unraveled.

What was thought obscure, perplexed and too hard for our weak parts, will lie open to the understanding in a fair view.

**2.** To embarrass; to puzzle; to distract; to tease with suspense, anxiety or ambiguity.

We can distinguish no general truths, or at least shall be apt to _perplex_ the mind.

We are perplexed, but not in despair. [[2 Corinthians 4#8|2 Corinthians 4:8]].

**3.** To plague; to vex.

**PERPLEX'**, _adjective_ Intricate; difficult. \[Not used.\]

# Vitiate

**VI'TIATE**, _verb transitive_ \[Latin vitio. See [[vice and Viciate]].\]

**1.** To injure the substance or qualities of a thing, so as to impair or spoil its use and value. Thus we say, luxury vitiates the humors of the body; evil examples _vitiate_ the morals of youth; language is vitiated by foreign idioms.

This undistinguishing complaisance will _vitiate_ the taste of readers.

**2.** To render defective; to destroy; as the validity or binding force of an instrument or transaction. Any undue influence exerted on a jury vitiates their verdict. Fraud vitiates a contract.

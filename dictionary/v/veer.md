# Veer

**VEER**, _verb intransitive_ \[Latin vario and verto. See [[Ware]].\]

To turn; to change direction; as, the wind veers to the west or north.

And as he leads, the following navy veers.

And turn your veering heart with ev'ry gale.

To _veer_ and haul, as wind, to alter its direction.

**VEER**, _verb transitive_ To turn; to direct to a different course.

To _veer_ out, to suffer to run or to let out to a greater length; as, to veery out a rope.

To _veer_ away, to let out; to slacken and let run; as, to _veer_ away the cable. this is called also paying out the cable.

To _veer_ and haul, to pull tight and slacken alternately.

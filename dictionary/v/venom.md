# Venom

**VEN'OM**, _noun_ \[Latin venenum, venor, to hunt, to drive or chase; venio, to come. See [[Venus]], etc.\]

**1.** Poison; matter fatal or injurious to life. _venom_ is generally used to express noxious matter that is applied externally, or that is discharged from animals, as that of bites and stings of serpents, scorpions, etc.; and poison, to express substances taken into the stomach.

**2.** Spite; malice.

**VEN'OM**, _verb transitive_ To poison; to infect with _venom_ \[Little used, but envenom is in use and elegant. _venom_ may be elegantly used in poetry.\]

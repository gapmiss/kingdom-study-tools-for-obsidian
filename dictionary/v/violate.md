# Violate

**VI'OLATE**, _verb transitive_ \[Latin violo.\]

**1.** To injure; to hurt; to interrupt; to disturb; as, to _violate_ sleep.

Kindness for man, and pity for his fate, may mix with bliss and yet not _violate_

**2.** To break; to infringe; to transgress; as, to _violate_ the laws of the state, or the rules of good breeding; to _violate_ the divine commands; to _violate_ one's vows or promises. Promises and commands may be violated negatively, by non-observance.

**3.** To injure; to do violence to.

Forbid to _violate_ the sacred fruit.

**4.** To treat with irreverence; to profane; as, to _violate_ the sanctity of a holy place.

**5.** To ravish; to compress by force.

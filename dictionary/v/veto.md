# Veto

**VE'TO**, _noun_ \[Latin _veto_ I forbid.\] A forbidding; prohibition; or the right of forbidding; applied to the right of a king or other magistrate or officer to withhold his assent to the enactment of a law, or the passing of a decree. Thus the king of Great Britain has a _veto_ upon every act of parliament; he sometimes prevents the passing of a law by his vet.

# Vacant

**VA'CANT**, _adjective_ \[Latin vacans.\]

**1.** Empty; not filled; void of every substance except air; as a _vacant_ space between houses; _vacant_ room.

**2.** Empty; exhausted of air; as a _vacant_ receiver.

**3.** Free; unincumbered; unengaged with business or care.

Philosophy is the interest of those only who are _vacant_ from the affairs of the world.

**4.** Not filled or occupied with an incumbent or possessor; as a _vacant_ throne; a _vacant_ parish.

**5.** Being unoccupied with business; as _vacant_ hours; _vacant_ moments.

**6.** Empty of thought; thoughtless; not occupied with study or reflection; as a _vacant_ mind.

**7.** Indicating want of thought.

The duke had a pleasant and _vacant_ face.

**8.** In law, abandoned; having no heir; as _vacant_ effect or goods.

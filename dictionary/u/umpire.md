# Umpire

**UM'PIRE**, _noun_ \[Latin imperium, contracted, as in empire.\]

**1.** A third person called in to decide a controversy or question submitted to arbitrators, when the arbitrators do not agree in opinion.

**2.** A person to whose sole decision a controversy or question between parties is referred. Thus the emperor of Russia was constituted _umpire_ between Great Britain and the United States, to decide the controversy respecting the slaves carried from the states by the British troops.

**UM'PIRE**, _verb transitive_ To arbitrate; to decide as umpire; to settle, as a dispute. \[Little used.\]

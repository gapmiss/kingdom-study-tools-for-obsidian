# Upland

**UP'LAND**, _noun_ \[up and land.\] High land; ground elevated above the meadows and intervals which lie on the banks of rivers, near the sea, or between hills; land which is generally dry. It is opposed to meadow, march, swamp, interval, etc. Uplands are particularly valuable as affording pasture for sheep.

**UP'LAND**, _adjective_

**1.** Higher in situation; being on upland; as _upland_ inhabitants.

**2.** Pertaining to uplands; as _upland_ pasturage.

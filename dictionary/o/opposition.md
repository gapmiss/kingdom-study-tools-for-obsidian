# Opposition

**OPPOSI'TION**, _noun_ \[Latin oppositio.\]

**1.** Situation so as to front something else; a standing over against; as the _opposition_ of two mountains or buildings.

**2.** The act of opposing; attempt to check, restrain or defeat. he makes _opposition_ to the measure; the bill passed without _opposition_ Will any _opposition_ be made to the suit, to the claim or demand?

**3.** Obstacle. the river meets with no _opposition_ in its course to the ocean.

**4.** Resistance; as the _opposition_ of enemies. Virtue will break through all _opposition_

**5.** Contrariety; repugnance in principle; as the _opposition_ of the heart to the laws of God.

**6.** Contrariety of interests, measures on designs. The two parties are in _opposition_ to each other.

**7.** Contrariety or diversity of meaning; as one term used in _opposition_ to another.

**8.** Contradiction; inconsistency.

**9.** The collective body of opposers; in England, the party in Parliament which opposed the ministry; in America, the party that opposed the existing administration.

**10.** In astronomy, the situation of two heavenly bodies, when distant from each other 180 degrees.

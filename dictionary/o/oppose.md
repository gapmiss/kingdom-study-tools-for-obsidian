# Oppose

**OPPO'SE**, _verb transitive_ s as z. \[Latin oppono, opposui. The change of n into s is unusual. Two different verbs may be used, as in Latin fero, tuli. See [[Pose]].\]

**1.** To set; against; to put in opposition, with a view to counterbalance or countervail, and thus to hinder defeat, destroy or prevent effect; as, to _oppose_ one argument to another.

I may without presumption _oppose_ my single opinion to his.

**2.** To act against; to resist, either by physical means, by arguments or other means. The army opposed the progress of the enemy, but without success. Several members of the house strenuously opposed the bill, but it passed.

**3.** To check; to resist effectually. The army was not able to _oppose_ the progress of the enemy.

**4.** To place in front; to set opposite.

**5.** To act against, as a competitor.

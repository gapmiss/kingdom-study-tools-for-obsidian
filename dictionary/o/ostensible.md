# Ostensible

**OSTEN'SIBLE**, _adjective_ \[Latin ostendo, to show.\]

**1.** That may be shown; proper or intended to be shown.

**2.** Plausible; colorable.

**3.** Appearing; seeming; shown, declared or avowed. We say, the _ostensible_ reason or motive for a measure may be the real one, or very different from the real one. This is the common, and I believe the only sense in which the word is used in America.

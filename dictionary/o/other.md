# Other

**OTH'ER**, _adjective_ \[Heb.\]

**1.** Not the same; different; not this or these.

Then the _other_ company which is left shall escape. [[Genesis 32#8|Genesis 32:8]].

Behold, it was turned again as his _other_ flesh. [[Exodus 4#7|Exodus 4:7]].

Other lords besides thee have had dominion over us. [[Isaiah 26#13|Isaiah 26:13]].

There is one God, and there is none _other_ but he. [[Mark 12#31|Mark 12:31]].

**2.** Not this, but the contrary; as, on this side of the river stands Troy, on the _other_ side stands Albany.

Whosoever shall smite thee on thy right cheek, turn to him the _other_ also. [[Matthew 5#39|Matthew 5:39]].

**3.** Noting something besides. To the knowledge of the Latin and Greek, join as much _other_ learning as you can.

**4.** Correlative to each, and applicable to any number of individuals.

They asked each _other_ of their welfare. [[Exodus 18#4|Exodus 18:4]].

**5.** Opposed to some; as, 'some fell among thorns - but _other_ fell into good ground.' [[Matthew 13#8|Matthew 13:8]].

**6.** The next.

**7.** The third part.

Other is used as a substitute for a noun, and in this use has the plural number, and the sign of the possessive case.

\- The fool and the brutish person die, and leave their wealth to others. [[Psalms 49#10|Psalms 49:10]].

What do ye more than others? [[Matthew 5#39|Matthew 5:39]].

We were children of wrath even as others. [[Ephesians 2#3|Ephesians 2:3]].

The confusion arises, when the one will put their sickle into the other's harvest.

With the sign of the possessive, _other_ is preceded by the, as in the last example.

Other is sometimes put elliptically for _other_ thing. From such a man, we can expect no _other_

The _other_ day, at a certain time past, not distant but indefinite; not long ago.

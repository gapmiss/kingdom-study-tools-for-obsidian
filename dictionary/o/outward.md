# Outward

**OUT'WARD**, _adjective_ \[Latin versus.\]

**1.** External; exterior; forming the superficial part; as the _outward_ coat of an onion; an _outward_ garment.

**2.** External; visible; opposed to inward; as _outward_ hate.

**3.** Extrinsic; adventitious.

And _outward_ honor for an inward toil.

**4.** Foreign; not intestine; as an _outward_ war. \[Not now used. We now say, external or foreign war.\]

**5.** Tending to the exterior part.

The fire will force its _outward_ way.

**6.** In Scripture, civil; public; as opposed to religious. [[1 Chronicles 24#1|1 Chronicles 24:1]].

**7.** In theology, carnal; fleshly; corporeal; not spiritual; as the _outward_ man.

**OUT'WARD**, _noun_ External form.

# Observer

**OBSERV'ER**, _noun_ s as z.

**1.** One who observes; one that takes notice; particularly, one who looks to with care, attention or vigilance.

Careful observers may foretell the hour, by sure prognostic, when to dread a shower.

Creditors are great observers of set days and times.

**2.** A beholder; a looker on; a spectator.

**3.** One who keeps any law, custom, regulation or rite; one who adheres to any thing in practice; one who performs; as a great _observer_ of forms; an _observer_ of old customs.

**4.** One who fulfills or performs; as, he is a strict _observer_ of his word or promise.

**5.** One who keeps religiously; as an _observer_ of the sabbath.

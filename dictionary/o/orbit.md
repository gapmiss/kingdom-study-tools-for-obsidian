# Orbit

**ORB'IT**, _noun_ \[Latin orbita, a trace or track, from orbis, a wheel.\]

**1.** In astronomy, the path of a planet or comet; the curve line which a planet describes in its periodical revolution round its central body; as the _orbit_ of Jupiter or Mercury. The _orbit_ of the earth is nearly one hundred and ninety millions of miles in diameter. The _orbit_ of the moon is 480, 000 miles in diameter. The orbits of the planets are elliptical.

**2.** A small orb. \[Not proper.\]

**3.** In anatomy, the cavity in which the eye is situated.

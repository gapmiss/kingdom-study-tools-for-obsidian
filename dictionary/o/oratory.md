# Oratory

**OR'ATORY**, _noun_ \[Low Latin oratoria, from orator.\]

**1.** The art of speaking well, or of speaking according to the rules of rhetoric, in order to persuade. To constitute _oratory_ the speaking must be just and pertinent to the subject; it must be methodical, all parts of the discourse being disposed in due order and connection; and it must be embellished with the beauties of language and pronounced with eloquence. _oratory_ consists of four parts, invention, disposition, elocution, and pronunciation.

**2.** Exercise of eloquence.

**3.** Among the Romanists, a close apartment near a bed-chamber, furnished with an altar, a crucifix, etc. for private devotions.

**4.** A place allotted for prayer, or a place for public worship.

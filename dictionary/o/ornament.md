# Ornament

**OR'NAMENT**, _noun_ \[Latin ornamentum, from orno, to adorn. Varro informs us that this was primitively osnamentum; but this is improbable. See [[Adorn]].\]

**1.** That which embellishes; something which, added to another thing, renders it more beautiful to the eye.

The chains, and the bracelets, and the mufflers, the bonnets and the ornaments of the legs - [[Isaiah 3#18|Isaiah 3:18]].

**2.** In architecture, ornaments are sculpture or carved work.

**3.** Embellishment; decoration; additional beauty.

\- The _ornament_ of a meek and quiet spirit which is in the sight of God of great price. [[1 Peter 3#4|1 Peter 3:4]].

**OR'NAMENT**, _verb transitive_ To adorn; to deck; to embellish.

# Overt

**O'VERT**, _adjective_ \[Latin aperio.\]

Open to view; public; apparent; as _overt_ virtues; an _overt_ essay. But the word is now used chiefly in law. Thus an _overt_ act of treason is distinguished from secret design or intention not carried into effect, and even from words spoken. A market _overt_ is a place where goods are publicly exposed to sale. A pound over, is one open overhead, as distinguished from a pound covert or close.

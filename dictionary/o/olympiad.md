# Olympiad

**OLYM'PIAD**, _noun_ \[Latin Olympias; Gr. from Olympus, a mountain of Macedonia.\]

A period of four years reckoned from one celebration of the Olympic games to another, and constituting an important epoch in history and chronology. The first _olympiad_ commenced 775 years before the birth of Christ, and 22 years before the foundation of Rome. The computation by Olympiads ceased at the three hundred and sixty fourth _olympiad_ in the year 440 of the christian era.

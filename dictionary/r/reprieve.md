# Reprieve

**REPRIE'VE**, _verb transitive_ \[I know not the origin of this word.\]

**1.** To respit after sentence of death; to suspend or delay the execution of for a time; as, to _reprieve_ a criminal for thirty days.

He reprieves the sinner from time to time.

**2.** To grant a respit to; to relieve for a time for any suffering.

Company, though it may _reprieve_ a man from his melancholy, yet cannot secure a man from his conscience.

**REPRIE'VE**, _noun_

**1.** The temporary suspension of the execution of sentence of death on a criminal.

**2.** Respit; interval of ease or relief.

All that I ask is but a short _reprieve_ till I forgot to love, and learn to grieve.

# Reservation

**RESERVA'TION**, _noun_ s as z. \[Latin reservo.\]

**1.** The act of reserving or keeping back or in the mind; reserve; concealment or withholding from disclosure; as mental _reservation_

**2.** Something withheld, either not expressed or disclosed, or not given up or brought forward.

With _reservation_ of a hundred knights.

In the United States, a tract of land not sold with the rest, is called a _reservation_

**3.** Custody; state of being treasured up or kept in store.

**4.** In law, a clause or part of an instrument by which something is reserved, not conceded or granted; also, a proviso.

Mental _reservation_ is the withholding of expression or disclosure of something that affects a proposition or statement, and which if disclosed, would materially vary its import.

Mental reservations are the refuge of hypocrites.

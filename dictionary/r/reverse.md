# Reverse

**REVERSE**, _verb transitive_ revers'. \[Latin reversus, reverto; re and verto, to turn.\]

**1.** To turn upside down; as, to _reverse_ a pyramid or cone.

**2.** To overturn; to subvert; as, to _reverse_ the state.

**3.** To turn back; as with swift wheel _reverse_

**4.** To turn to the contrary; as, to _reverse_ the scene.

\- Or affectations quite _reverse_ the soul.

**5.** To put each in the place of the other; as, to _reverse_ the distinctions of good and evil.

**6.** In law, to overthrow by a contrary decision; to make void; to annul; as, to _reverse_ a judgment, sentence or decree. Judgments are reversed by writs of error; and for certain causes, may be reversed without such writs.

**7.** To recall. \[Not in use.\]

**REVERSE**, _verb intransitive_ revers'. To return. \[Not in use.\]

**REVERSE**, _noun_ revers'.

**1.** Change; vicissitude; a turn of affairs; in a good sense.

By a strange _reverse_ of things, Justinian's law, which for many ages was neglected, now obtains -

**2.** Change for the worse; misfortune. By an unexpected _reverse_ of circumstances, an affluent man is reduced to poverty.

**3.** A contrary; an opposite.

The performances to which God has annexed the promises of eternity, are just the _reverse_ of all the pursuits of sense.

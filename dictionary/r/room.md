# Room

**ROOM**, _noun_

**1.** Space; compass; extent of place, great or small. Let the words occupy as little _room_ as possible.

**2.** Space or place unoccupied.

Lord, it is done as thou hast commanded, and yet there is _room_ [[Luke 14#8|Luke 14:8]].

**3.** Place for reception or admission of any thing. In this case, there is no _room_ for doubt or for argument.

**4.** Place of another; stead; as in succession or substitution. One magistrate or king comes in the _room_ of a former one. We often place one thing in the _room_ of another. [[1 Kings 20#24|1 Kings 20:24]].

**5.** Unoccupied opportunity. The eager pursuit of wealth leaves little _room_ for serious reflection.

**6.** An apartment in a house; any division separated from the rest by a partition; as a parlor, drawing _room_ or bed-room; also an apartment in a ship, as the cook-room, bread-room, gun-room, etc.

**7.** A seat. [[Luke 14#8|Luke 14:8]].

To make _room_ to open a way or passage; to free from obstructions.

To make _room_ to open a space or place for any thing.

To give _room_ to withdraw; to leave space unoccupied for others to pass or to be seated.

**ROOM**, _verb intransitive_ To occupy an apartment; to lodge; an academic use of the word. A B rooms at No. 7.

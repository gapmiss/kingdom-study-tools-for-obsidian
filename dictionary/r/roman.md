# Roman

**RO'MAN**, _adjective_ \[Latin Romanus, from Roma, the principal city of the Romans in Italy. Rome is the oriental name Ramah, elevated, that is, a hill; for fortresses and towns were often placed on hills for security; Heb. to be high, to raise.\]

**1.** Pertaining to Rome, or to the _roman_ people.

**2.** Romish; popish; professing the religion of the pope.

Roman catholic, as an adjective, denoting the religion professed by the people of Rome and of Italy, at the head of which is the pope or bishop of Rome; as a noun, one who adheres to the papal religion.

**RO'MAN**, _noun_

**1.** A native of rome.

**2.** A citizen of Rome; one enjoying the privileges of a _roman_ citizen.

**3.** One of the christian church at Rome to which Paul addressed an epistle, consisting of converts from Judaism or paganism.

# Repel

**REPEL**', _verb transitive_ \[Latin repello; re and pello, to drive.\]

**1.** to drive back; to force to return; to check advance; as, to _repel_ an enemy or an assailant.

Hippomedon repell'd the hostile tide.

And virtue may _repel_ though not invade.

**2.** To resist; to oppose; as, to _repel_ an argument.

**REPEL'**, _verb intransitive_

**1.** To act with force in opposition to force impressed. Electricity sometimes attracts and sometimes repels.

**2.** In medicine, to check an afflux to a part of the body.

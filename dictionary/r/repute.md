# Repute

**REPU'TE**, _verb transitive_ \[Latin reputo; re and puto, to think.\]

To think; to account; to hold; to reckon.

The king was reputed a prince most prudent.

Wherefore are we counted as beasts, and reputed vile in your sight. [[Job 18#3|Job 18:3]].

**REPU'TE**, _noun_

**1.** Reputation; good character; the credit or honor derived from common or public opinion; as men of _repute_

**2.** Character; in a bad sense; as a man held in bad _repute_

**3.** Established opinion; as upheld by old _repute_

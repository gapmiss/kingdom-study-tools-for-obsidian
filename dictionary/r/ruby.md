# Ruby

**RU'BY**, _noun_ \[Latin rubeo, to be red.\]

**1.** A precious stone; a mineral of a carmine red color, sometimes verging to violet, or intermediate between carmine and hyacinth red; but its parts vary in color, and hence it is called sapphire _ruby_ or orange red, and by some vermeille or rubicel.

There are two kinds of _ruby_ the oriental or corundum, and the spinelle. The latter is distinguishable from the former by its color and crystallization.

The _ruby_ is next in hardness and value to the diamond, and highly esteemed in jewelry.

**2.** Redness; red color.

**3.** Any thing red.

**4.** A blain; a blotch; a carbuncle. \[The _ruby_ is said to be the stone called by Pliny a carbuncle.\]

Ruby of arsenic or sulphur, is the realgar, or red combination of arsenic and sulphur.

Ruby of zink, is the red blend.

Rock _ruby_ the amethystizontes of the ancients, is the most value species of garnet.

**RU'BY**, _verb transitive_ To make red.

**RU'BY**, _adjective_ Of the color of the ruby; red; as _ruby_ lips.

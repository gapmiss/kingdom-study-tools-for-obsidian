# Reasonable

**RE'ASONABLE**, _adjective_

**1.** Having the faculty of reason; endued with reason; as a _reasonable_ being. \[In this sense, rational is now generally used.\]

**2.** Governed by reason; being under the influence of reason; thinking, speaking or acting rationally or according to the dictates of reason; as, the measure must satisfy all _reasonable_ men.

**3.** Conformable or agreeable to reason; just; rational.

By indubitable certainty, I mean that which does not admit of any _reasonable_ cause of doubting.

A law may be _reasonable_ in itself, though a man does not allow it.

**4.** Not immoderate.

Let all things be thought upon, that may with _reasonable_ swiftness add more feathers to our wings.

**5.** Tolerable; being in mediocrity; moderate; as a _reasonable_ quantity.

**6.** Not excessive; not unjust; as a _reasonable_ fine; a _reasonable_ sum in damages.

# Recreate

**REC'REATE**, _verb transitive_ \[Latin recero; re and creo, to create.\]

**1.** To refresh after toil; to reanimate, as languid spirits or exhausted strength; to amuse or divert in weariness.

Painters when they work on white grounds, place before them colors mixed with blue and green, to _recreate_ their eyes.

St. John is said to have recreated himself with sporting with a tame partridge.

**2.** To gratify; to delight.

These ripe fruits _recreate_ the nostrils with their aromatic scent.

**3.** To relieve; to revive; as, to _recreate_ the lungs with fresh air.

**REC'REATE**, _verb intransitive_ To take recreation.

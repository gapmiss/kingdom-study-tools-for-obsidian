# Rook

**ROOK**, _noun_ \[Latin graculus; probably from its voice. See [[Crow and Croak]].\]

**1.** A fowl of the genus Corvus, the fowl mentioned by Virgil under this name. This fowl resembles the crow, but differs from it in not feeding on carrion, but on insects and grain. In crows also the nostrils and root of the bill are clothed with feathers, but in rooks the same parts are naked, or have only a few bristly hairs. The _rook_ is gregarious.

**2.** A cheat; a trickish, rapacious fellow.

**ROOK**, _noun_ A common man at chess.

**ROOK**, _verb intransitive_ To cheat; to defraud.

**ROOK**, _verb transitive_ To cheat; to defraud by cheating.

**ROOK**, _verb intransitive_ To squat. \[See Ruck.\]

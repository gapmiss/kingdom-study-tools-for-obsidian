# Ram

**RAM**, _noun_ \[See the Verb.\]

**1.** The male of the sheep or ovine genus; in some parts of England called a tup. In the United States, the word is applied, I believe, to no other male, except in the compound ram-cat.

**2.** In astronomy, Aries, the sign of the zodiac which the sun enters on the 21st of March, or a constellation of fixed stars in the figure of a _ram_ It is considered the first of the twelve signs.

**3.** An engine of war, used formerly for battering and demolishing the walls of cities; called a battering-ram. \[See Battering-ram.\]

**RAM**, _verb transitive_ \[Latin ramus, a branch that is a shoot or thrust. Heb. See Cram.\]

**1.** To thrust or drive with violence; to force in; to drive down or together; as, to _ram_ down a cartridge; to _ram_ piles into the earth.

**2.** To drive, as with a battering _ram_

**3.** To stuff; to cram.

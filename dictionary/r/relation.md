# Relation

**RELA'TION**, _noun_ \[Latin relatio, refero.\]

**1.** The act of telling; recital; account; narration; narrative of facts; as a historical _relation_ We listened to the _relation_ of his adventures.

**2.** Respect; reference; regard.

I have been importuned to make some observations on this art, in _relation_ to its agreement with poetry.

**3.** Connection between things; mutual respect, or what one thing is with regard to another; as the _relation_ of a citizen to the state; the _relation_ of a subject to the supreme authority; the _relation_ of husband and wife, or of master and servant; the _relation_ of a state of probation to a state of retribution.

**4.** Kindred; alliance; as the _relation_ of parents and children.

Relations dear, and all the charities of father, son and brother, first were known.

**5.** A person connected by consanguinity or affinity; a kinsman or kinswoman. He passed a month with his relations in the country.

**6.** Resemblance of phenomena; analogy.

**7.** In geometry, ratio; proportion.

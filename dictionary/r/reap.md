# Reap

**REAP**, _verb transitive_ \[Latin rapio, carpo; Gr. a sickle, to reap; Eng. crop.\]

**1.** To cut grain with a sickle; as, to _reap_ wheat or rye.

When ye _reap_ the harvest, thou shalt not wholly _reap_ the corners of thy field. [[Leviticus 19#9|Leviticus 19:9]].

**2.** To clear of a crop by reaping; as, to _reap_ a field.

**3.** To gather; to obtain; to receive as a reward, or as the fruit of labor or of works; in a good or bad sense; as, to _reap_ a benefit from exertions.

He that soweth to the flesh, shall of the flesh _reap_ corruption. [[Galatians 6#7|Galatians 6:7]].

Ye have plowed wickedness; ye have reaped iniquity.

Hosea 10.

**REAP**, _verb intransitive_

**1.** To perform the act or operation of reaping. In New England, farmers _reap_ in July and August.

**2.** To receive the fruit of labor or works.

They that sow in tears, shall _reap_ in joy. [[Psalms 126#5|Psalms 126:5]].

# Relax

**RELAX**', _verb transitive_ \[Latin relaxo; re and laxo, to slacken.\]

**1.** To slacken; to make less tense or rigid; as, to _relax_ a rope or cord; to _relax_ the muscles or sinews; to _relax_ the reins in riding.

**2.** To loosen; to make less close or firm; as, to _relax_ the joints.

**3.** To make less severe or rigorous; to remit or abate in strictness; as, to _relax_ a law or rule of justice; to _relax_ a demand.

**4.** To remit or abate in attention, assiduity or labor; as, to _relax_ study; to _relax_ exertions or efforts.

**5.** To unbend; to ease; to relieve from close attention; as, conversation relaxes the student or the mind.

**6.** To relieve from constipation; to loosen; to open; as, medicines _relax_ the bowels.

**7.** To open; to loose.

**8.** To make languid.

**RELAX'**, _verb intransitive_

**1.** To abate in severity; to become more mild or less rigorous.

In others she relax'd again, and govern'd with a looser rein.

**2.** To remit in close attention. It is useful for the student to _relax_ often, and give himself to exercise and amusements.

**RELAX'**, _noun_ Relaxation. \[Not used.\]

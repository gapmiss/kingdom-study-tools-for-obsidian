# Revenue

**REV'ENUE**, _noun_ \[Latin revenio; re and venio, to come.\]

**1.** In a general sense, the annual rents, profits, interest or issues of any species of property, real or personal, belonging to an individual or to the public. When used of individuals, it is equivalent to income. In modern usage, income is applied more generally to the rents and profits of individuals, and _revenue_ to those of the state. In the latter case, _revenue_ is

**2.** The annual produce of taxes, excise, customs, duties, rents, etc. which a nation or state collects and receives into the treasury for public use.

**3.** Return; reward; as a rich _revenue_ of praise.

**4.** A fleshy lump on the head of a deer.

# Resume

**RES'UME**, _verb transitive_ s as z. \[Latin resumo; re and sumo, to take.\]

**1.** To take back what has been given.

The sun, like this from which our sight we have, gaz'd on too long, resumes the light he gave.

**2.** To take back what has been taken away.

They _resume_ what has been obtained fraudulently.

**3.** To take again after absence; as, to _resume_ a seat.

Reason resum'd her place, and passion fled.

**4.** To take up again after interruption; to begin again; as, to _resume_ an argument or discourse. \[This is now its most frequent use.\]

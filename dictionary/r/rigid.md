# Rigid

**RIG'ID**, _adjective_ \[Gr. to be stiff; Latin frigeo, frigidus; Heb. to be still, to be stiff.\]

**1.** Stiff; not pliant; not easily bent. It is applied to bodies or substances that are naturally soft or flexible, but not fluid. We never say, a _rigid_ stone or _rigid_ iron, nor do we say, _rigid_ ice; but we say, an animal body or limb, when cold, is _rigid_ _rigid_ is then opposed to flexible, but expresses less than inflexible.

**2.** Strict in opinion, practice or discipline; severe in temper; opposed to lax or indulgent; as a _rigid_ father or master; a _rigid_ officer.

**3.** Strict; exact; as a _rigid_ law or rule; _rigid_ discipline; _rigid_ criticism.

**4.** Severely just; as a _rigid_ sentence or judgment.

**5.** Exactly according to the sentence or law; as _rigid_ execution.

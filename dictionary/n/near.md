# Near

**NEAR**, _adjective_ \[This seems to be a contracted word, from nigher, the comparative of neh, nih or nieh; strait, narrow; to narrow\]

**1.** Nigh; not far distant in place, time or degree. Regularly, _near_ should be followed by to, but this is often omitted. We say, a house stands _near_ a river; a friend sits _near_ me; the man fell and was _near_ destruction.

And Jacob went _near_ to Isaac his father. [[Genesis 27#21|Genesis 27:21]].

Now is our salvation nearer than when we believed. [[Romans 13#11|Romans 13:11]].

**2.** Closely related by blood.

She is thy fathers _near_ kinswoman. [[Leviticus 18#6|Leviticus 18:6]].

**3.** Not distant in affection, support or assistance; present; ready; willing to aid.

Call upon the Lord, while he is _near_ [[Isaiah 55#6|Isaiah 55:6]].

**4.** Intimate; united in close ties of affection or confidence; as a _near_ friend.

**5.** Dear; affecting ones interest or feelings; as a _near_ concern.

My nearest life.

**6.** Close; parsimonious.

**7.** Close; not loose, free or rambling; as a version _near_ the original.

**8.** Next to one; opposed to off; as the _near_ horse or ox in a team.

**NEAR**, _adverb_ Almost; within a little. It is _near_ twelve oclock. The payment of such a sum would go _near_ to ruin him.

**NEAR**, _verb transitive_ To approach; to come nearer; as, the ship neared the land; a seamans phrase.

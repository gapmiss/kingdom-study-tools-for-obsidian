# Net

**NET**, _noun_

**1.** An instrument for catching fish and fowls, or wild beasts, formed with twine or thread interwoven with meshes.

**2.** A cunning device; a snare. Mich 7.

**3.** Inextricable difficulty. [[Job 18#8|Job 18:8]].

**4.** Severe afflictions. [[Job 19#6|Job 19:6]].

**NET**, _verb transitive_ To make a _net_ or net-work; to knot.

**NET**, _adjective_ \[See [[Neat]]\]

**1.** Neat; pure; unadulterated.

**2.** Being without flaw or spot.

**3.** Being beyond all charges or outlay; as _net_ profits.

**4.** Being clear of all tare and tret, or all deductions; as _net_ weight. It is sometimes written nett, but improperly. _net_ is properly a mercantile appropriation of neat.

**NET**, _verb transitive_ To produce clear profit.

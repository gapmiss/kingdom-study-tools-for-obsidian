# Welk

**WELK**, _verb intransitive_ \[G., to wither, to fade, to decay; primarily to shrink or contract, as things in drying, whence the Saxon weole, a whilk or whelk, a shell; from its wrinkles.\] To decline; to fade; to decay; to fall.

When ruddy Phoebus gins to _welk_ in west.

**WELK**, _verb transitive_ To contract; to shorten.

Now sad winter welked hath the day-- \[This word is obsolete. But its signification has heretofore been misunderstood.\]

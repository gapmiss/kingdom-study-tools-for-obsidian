# Weight

**WEIGHT**, _noun_ Wate. \[See [[Weigh]].\]

**1.** The quantity of a body, ascertained by the balance; in a philosophical sense, that quality of bodies by which they tend towards the center of the earth in a line perpendicular to its surface. In short, _weight_ is gravity, and the _weight_ of a particular body is the amount of its gravity, or of the force with which it tends to the center. The _weight_ of a body is in direct proportion to its quantity of matter.

**2.** A mass of iron, lead, brass or other metal, to be used for ascertaining the _weight_ of other bodies; as a _weight_ of an ounce, a pound, a quarter of a hundred, etc. The weights of nations are different except those of England and the United States, which are the same.

**3.** A ponderous mass; something heavy.

A man leaps better with weights in his hands.

**4.** Pressure; burden; as the _weight_ of grief; _weight_ of care; _weight_ of business; _weight_ of government.

**5.** Importance; power; influence; efficacy; consequence; moment; impressiveness; as a argument of great weight; a consideration of vast _weight_ The dignity of a mans character adds _weight_ to his words.

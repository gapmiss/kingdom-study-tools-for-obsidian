# While

**WHILE**, _noun_ \[G. See the Verb.\] Time; space of time, or continued duration. He was some _while_ in this country. One _while_ we thought him innocent.

Pausing a _while_ thus to herself she musd.

Worth _while_ worth the time which it requires; worth the time and pains; hence, worth the expense. It is not always worth _while_ for a man to prosecute for small debts.

**WHILE**, _adverb_

**1.** During the time that _while_ I write, you sleep.

**2.** As long as.

Use your memory, and you will sensibly experience a gradual improvement, _while_ you take care not to overload it.

**3.** At the same time that.

**WHILE**, _verb transitive_ \[G., to abide, to stay.\] To _while_ away, as time, in English, is to loiter; or more generally, to cause time to pass away pleasantly, without irksomeness; as, we _while_ away time in amusements or diversions.

Let us _while_ away this life.

**WHILE**, _verb intransitive_ To loiter.

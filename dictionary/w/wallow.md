# Wallow

**WALLOW**, _verb intransitive_ \[Latin , G. This verb seems to be connected with well, walk, etc.\]

**1.** To roll ones body on the earth, in mire, or on other substance; to tumble and roll in water. Swine _wallow_ in the mire.

**2.** To move heavily and clumsily.

Part huge of bulk, wallowing unwieldy, enormous in their gait, tempest the ocean. \[Unusual.\]

**3.** To live in filth or gross vice; as man wallowing in his native impurity.

**WALLOW**, _verb transitive_ To roll ones body.

**WALLOW** thyself in ashes. [[Jeremiah 6#26|Jeremiah 6:26]].

**WALLOW**, _noun_ A kind of rolling walk.

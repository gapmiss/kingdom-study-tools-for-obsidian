# Worse

**WORSE**, _adjective_ \[This adjective has the signification of the comparative degree, and as bad has no comparative and superlative, _worse_ and worst are used in lieu of them, although radically they have no relation to bad.\]

**1.** More evil; more bad or ill; more depraved and corrupt; in a moral sense.

Evil men and seducers shall wax _worse_ and _worse_ [[2 Timothy 3#13|2 Timothy 3:13]].

There are men who seem to believe they are not bad, while another can be found _worse_

**2.** In a physical sense, in regard to health, more sick.

She was nothing bettered, but rather grew _worse_ [[Mark 5#26|Mark 5:26]].

**3.** More bad; less perfect or good. This carriage is _worse_ for wear.

The _worse_

**1.** The loss; the disadvantage.

Judah was put to the _worse_ before Israel. [[2 Kings 14#12|2 Kings 14:12]].

**2.** Something less good. Think not the _worse_ of him for his enterprise.

**WORSE**, _adverb_ In a manner more evil or bad.

We will deal _worse_ with thee than with them. [[Genesis 19#9|Genesis 19:9]].

**WORSE**, to put to disadvantage, is not in use. \[See [[Worst]].\]

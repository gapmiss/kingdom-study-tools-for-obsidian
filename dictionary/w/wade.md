# Wade

**WADE**, _verb intransitive_

**1.** To walk through any substance that yields to the feet; as, to _wade_ through water; to _wade_ through sand or snow; . To _wade_ over a river, is to walk through on the bottom. Fowls that _wade_ have long legs.

**2.** To move or pass with difficulty or labor; as, judges _wade_ through an intriccate law case. it is not my purpose to _wade_ through these controversies.

The kings admirable conduct has waded through all these difficulties.

And wades through fumes and gropes his way.

**WADE**, _verb transitive_ To pass by walking on the bottom; as, to _wade_ a river. \[this is a common expression, but elliptical for to _wade_ through a river.\]

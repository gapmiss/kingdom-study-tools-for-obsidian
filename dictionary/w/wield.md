# Wield

**WIELD**, _verb transitive_ \[Latin The primary sense of power and strength is to stretch or strain.\]

**1.** To use with full command or power, as a thing not too heavy for the holder; to manage; as, to _wield_ a sword; to _wield_ the scepter.

Part _wield_ their arms, part curb the foaming steed.

**2.** To use or employ with the hand.

Nothing but the influence of a civilized power could induce a savage to _wield_ a spade.

**3.** To handle; in an ironical sense.

Base Hungarian wight, wilt thou the spigot wield?

To _wield_ the scepter, to govern with supreme command.

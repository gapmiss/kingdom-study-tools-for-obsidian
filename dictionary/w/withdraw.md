# Withdraw

**WITHDRAW**, _verb transitive_ \[with and draw.\]

**1.** To take back; to take from.

It is impossible that God should _withdraw_ his presence from any thing.

We say, to _withdraw_ capital from a bank or stock in trade, to _withdraw_ aid or assistance.

**2.** To recall; to cause to retire or leave; to call back or away. France has withdrawn her troops from Spain.

**WITHDRAW**, _verb intransitive_ To retire; to retreat; to quit a company or place. We withdrew from the company at ten oclock.

She from her husband soft withdrew.

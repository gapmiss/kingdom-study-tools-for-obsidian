# Tallage

**TAL'LAGE**

TAL'LIAGE, _noun_ Anciently, a certain rate or tax paid by barons, knights and inferior tenants, towards the public expenses. When it was paid out of knight's fees, it was called scutage; when by cities and burghs, talliage; when upon lands not held by military tenure, hidage.

**TAL'LAGE**, _verb transitive_ To lay an impost.

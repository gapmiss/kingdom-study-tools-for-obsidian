# Trap

**TRAP**, _noun_

**1.** An engine that shuts suddenly or with a spring, used for taking game; as a _trap_ for foxes. A _trap_ is a very different thing from a snare; though the latter word may be used in a figurative sense for a _trap_

**2.** An engine for catching men. \[Not used in the U. States.\]

**3.** An ambush; a stratagem; any device by which men or other animals may be caught unawares.

Let their table be made a snare and a _trap_ [[Romans 11#9|Romans 11:9]].

**4.** A play in which a ball is driven with a stick.

**TRAP**, _noun_ In mineralogy, a name given to rocks characterized by a columnar form, or whose strata or beds have the form of steps or a series of stairs. Kirwan gives this name to two families of basalt. It is now employed to designate a rock or aggregate in which hornblend predominates, but it conveys no definite idea of any one species; and under this term are comprehended hornblend, hornblend slate, greenstone, greenstone slate, amygdaloid, basalt, wacky, clinkstone porphyry, and perhaps hypersthene rock, augite rock, and some varieties of sienite.

**TRAP**, _verb transitive_ To catch in a trap; as, to _trap_ foxes or beaver.

**1.** To ensnare; to take by stratagem.

I trapp'd the foe.

**2.** To adorn; to dress with ornaments. \[See [[Trappings]].\] \[the verb is little used.\]

**TRAP**, _verb intransitive_ To set traps for game; as, to _trap_ for beaver.

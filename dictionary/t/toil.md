# Toil

**TOIL**, _verb intransitive_ To labor; to work; to exert strength with pain and fatigue of body or mind, particularly of the body, with efforts of some continuance or duration.

Master, we have toiled all night and caught nothing. [[Luke 5#5|Luke 5:5]].

**TOIL**, _verb transitive_ To _toil_ out, to labor; to work out.

**TOIL**'d out my uncouth passage--

**1.** To weary; to overlabor; as toil'd with works of war.

\[Not in use nor proper.\]

**TOIL**, _noun_ Labor with pain and fatigue; labor that oppresses the body or mind. _toil_ may be the labor of the field or the workshop, or of the camp. What toils men endure for the acquisition of wealth, power and honor! [[Genesis 5#29|Genesis 5:29]].

**TOIL**, _noun_ \[Latin tela, a web; from spreading, extending or laying.\]

A net or snare; any thread, web or string spread for taking prey.

A fly falls into the toils of a spider.

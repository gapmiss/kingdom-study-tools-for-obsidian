# Tame

**TAME**, _adjective_

**1.** That has lost its native wildness and shyness; mild; accustomed to man; domestic; as a _tame_ deer; a _tame_ bird.

**2.** Crushed; subdued; depressed; spiritless.

And you, _tame_ slaves of the laborious plow.

**3.** Spiritless; unanimated; as a _tame_ poem. \[Not elegant nor in use.

**TAME**, _verb transitive_ \[Latin domo; Heb. to be silent, dumb.\]

**1.** To reclaim; to reduce from a wild to a domestic state; to make gentle and familiar; as, to _tame_ a wild beast.

**2.** To civilize; as, to _tame_ the ferocious inhabitants of the forest.

**3.** To subdue; to conquer; to depress; as, to _tame_ the pride or passions of youth.

**4.** To subdue; to repress; as wildness or licentiousness.

The tongue can no man _tame_ [[James 3#8|James 3:8]].

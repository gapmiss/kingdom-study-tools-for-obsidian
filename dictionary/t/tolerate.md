# Tolerate

**TOL'ERATE**, _verb transitive_ \[Latin tolero, from tollo, to lift.\] To suffer to be or to be done without prohibition or hinderance; to allow or permit negatively, by not preventing; not to restrain; as, to _tolerate_ opinions or practices. The protestant religion is tolerated in France, and the Roman Catholic in Great Britain.

Crying should not be tolerated in children.

The law of love tolerates no vice, and patronizes every virtue.

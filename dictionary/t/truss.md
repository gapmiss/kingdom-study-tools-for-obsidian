# Truss

**TRUSS**, _noun_

**1.** In a general sense, a bundle; as a _truss_ of hay or straw. A _truss_ of hay in England is half a hundred. A _truss_ of straw is of different weights in different places.

**2.** In surgery, a bandage or apparatus used in cases of ruptures, to keep up the reduced parts and hinder further protrusion, and for other purposes.

**3.** Among botanists, a _truss_ or bunch is a tuft of flowers formed at the top of the main stalk or stem of certain plants.

**4.** In navigation, a machine to pull a lower yard close to its mast and retain it firmly in that position.

**5.** \[See [[Trous]].\]

**TRUSS**, _verb transitive_ To bind or pack close.

**1.** To skewer; to make fast.

To _truss_ up, to strain; to make close or tight.

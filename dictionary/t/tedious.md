# Tedious

**TE'DIOUS**, _adjective_ \[Latin toedium.\]

**1.** Wearisome; tiresome from continuance, prolixity, or slowness which causes prolixity. We say, a man is _tedious_ in relating a story; a minister is _tedious_ in his sermon. We say also, a discourse is _tedious_ when it wearies by its length or dullness.

**2.** Slow; as a _tedious_ course.

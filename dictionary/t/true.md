# True

**TRUE**, _adjective_

**1.** Conformable to fact; being in accordance with the actual state of things; as a _true_ relation or narration; a _true_ history. A declaration is _true_ when it states the facts. In this sense, _true_ is opposed to false.

**2.** Genuine; pure; real; not counterfeit, adulterated or false; as _true_ balsam; the _true_ bark; _true_ love of country; a _true_ christian.

\--The _true_ light which lighteth every man that cometh into the world. [[John 1#9|John 1:9]].

**3.** Faithful; steady in adhering to friends, to promises, to a prince or to the state; loyal; not false, fickle or perfidious; as a _true_ friend; a _true_ lover; a man _true_ to his king, _true_ to his country, _true_ to his word; a husband _true_ to his wife; a wife _true_ to her husband; a servant _true_ to his master; an officer _true_ to his charge.

**4.** Free from falsehood; as a _true_ witness.

**5.** Honest; not fraudulent; as good men and _true_

If king Edward be as _true_ and just--

**6.** Exact; right to precision; conformable to a rule or pattern; as a _true_ copy; a _true_ likeness of the original.

**7.** Straight; right; as a _true_ line; the _true_ course of a ship.

**8.** Not false or pretended; real; as, Christ was the _true_ Messiah.

**9.** Rightful; as, George IV is the _true_ king of England.

# Tuition

**TUI'TION**, _noun_ \[Latin tuitio, from tueor, to see, behold, protect, etc.; Latin duco, to lead.\]

**1.** Guardianship; superintending care over a young person; the particular watch and care of a tutor or guardian over his pupil or ward.

**2.** More especially, instruction; the act or business of teaching the various branches of learning. We place our children under the preceptors of academies for _tuition_ \[This is now the common acceptation of the word.\]

**3.** The money paid for instruction. In our colleges, the _tuition_ is from thirty to forty dollars a year.

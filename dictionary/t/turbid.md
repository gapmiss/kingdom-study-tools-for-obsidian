# Turbid

**TUR'BID**, _adjective_ \[Latin turbidus, from turbo, to disturb, that is, to stir, to turn.\] Properly, having the lees disturbed; but in a more general sense, muddy; foul with extraneous matter; thick; not clear; used of liquids of any kind; as _turbid_ water; _turbid_ wine. Streams running on clay generally appear to be _turbid_ This is often the case with the river Seine.

# Trample

**TRAM'PLE**, _verb transitive_

**1.** To tread under foot; especially, to tread upon with pride, contempt, triumph or scorn.

Neither cast ye your pearls before swine, lest they _trample_ them under their feet. [[Matthew 7#6|Matthew 7:6]].

**2.** To tread down; to prostrate by treading; as, to _trample_ grass.

**3.** To treat with pride, contempt and insult.

**TRAM'PLE**, _verb intransitive_ To tread in contempt.

Diogenes trampled on Plato's pride with greater of his own.

**1.** To tread with force and rapidity.

**TRAM'PLE**, _noun_ The act of treading under foot with contempt.

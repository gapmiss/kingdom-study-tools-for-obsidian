# Turf

**TURF**, _noun_

**1.** That upper stratum of earth and vegetable mold, which is filled with the roots of grass and other small plants, so as to adhere and form a kind of mat. This is otherwise called sward and sod.

**2.** Peat; a peculiar kind of blackish, fibrous, vegetable, earthy substance, used as fuel. \[Dryden and Addison wrote turfs, in the plural. But when _turf_ or peat is cut into small pieces, the practice now is to call them turves.\]

**3.** Race-ground; or horse-racing.

The honors of the _turf_ are all our own.

**TURF**, _verb transitive_ To cover with _turf_ or sod; as, to _turf_ a bank or the border of a terrace.

**TURF'-COVERED**, _adjective_ Covered with _turf_

**TURF'-DRAIN**, _noun_ A drain filled with _turf_ or peat.

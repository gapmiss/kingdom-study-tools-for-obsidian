# Thirst

**THIRST**, _noun_ thurst. \[Latin torreo.\]

**1.** A painful sensation of the throat or fauces, occasioned by the want of drink.

Wherefore is it that thou hast brought us out of Egypt, to kill us and our children and our cattle with thirst? [[Exodus 17#3|Exodus 17:3]].

**2.** A vehement desire of drink. [[Psalms 104#11|Psalms 104:11]].

**3.** A want and eager desire after any thing.

**THIRST** of worldly good.

**THIRST** of knowledge.

**THIRST** of praise.

**THIRST** after happiness.

But for is now more generally used after thirst; as a _thirst_ for worldly honors; a _thirst_ for praise.

**4.** Dryness; drouth.

_The rapid current, through veins_

Of porous earth with kindly _thirst_ updrawn,

Rose a fresh fountain--

# Tack

**TACK**, _verb transitive_ \[Gr. to set, place, ordain.\]

**1.** To fasten; to attach. In the solemn or grave style, this word now appears ludicrous; as, to get a commendam tacked to their sees.

\--And _tack_ the center to the sphere.

**2.** To unite by stitching together; as, to _tack_ together the sheets of a book; to _tack_ one piece of cloth to another. \[In the familiar style, this word is in good use.\]

**3.** To fasten slightly by nails; as, to _tack_ on a board or shingle.

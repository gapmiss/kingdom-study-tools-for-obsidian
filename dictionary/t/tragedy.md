# Tragedy

**TRAG'EDY**, _noun_ \[Gr. said to be composed of a goat, and a song, because originally it consisted in a hymn sung in honor of Bacchus by a chorus of music, with dances and the sacrifice of a goat.\]

**1.** A dramatic poem representing some signal action performed by illustrious persons, and generally having a fatal issue. Aeschylus is called the father of _tragedy_

All our tragedies are of kings and princes.

**2.** A fatal and mournful event; any event in which human lives are lost by human violence, more particularly by unauthorized violence.

# Transmutation

**TRANSMUTA'TION**, _noun_ \[Latin transmutatio.\]

**1.** The change of any thing into another substance, or into something of a different nature. For a long time, the _transmutation_ of base metals into gold was deemed practicable, but nature proved refractory, and the alchimists were frustrated.

**2.** In chemistry, the _transmutation_ of one substance into another is very easy and common, as of water into gas or vapor, and of gases into water.

**3.** In geometry, the change or reduction of one figure or body into another of the same area or solidity, but of a different form; as of a triangle into a square.

**4.** The change of colors, as in the case of a decoction of the nephritic wood.

**5.** In the vegetable economy, the change of a plant into another form; as of wheat into chess, according to the popular opinion. \[See [[Chess]].\]

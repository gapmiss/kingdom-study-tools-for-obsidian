# Hanaper

**HAN'APER**, _noun_ The _hanaper_ was used in early days by the kings of England, for holding and carrying with them their money, as they journeyed from place to place. It was a kind of basket, like the fiscus, and hence came to be considered as the king's treasury. Hence, the clerk or warden of the _hanaper_ is an officer who receives the fees due to the king for seals of charters, patents, commissions, and writs. There is also an officer who is controller of the _hanaper_ This word therefore answered to the modern exchequer.

# Hanker

**HANK'ER**, _verb intransitive_

**1.** To long for with a keen appetite and uneasiness; in a literal sense; as, to _hanker_ for fruit, or after fruit.

**2.** To have a vehement desire of something, accompanied with uneasiness; as, to _hanker_ after the diversions of the town.

It is usually followed by after. It is a familiar, but not a low word.

# Hepatic

**HEPAT'IC**

**HEPAT'IC**AL, _adjective_ \[Latin hepaticus; Gr. the liver.\] Pertaining to the liver; as _hepatic_ gall; _hepatic_ pain; _hepatic_ artery; _hepatic_ flux.

Hepatic air or gas, is a fetid vapor or elastic fluid emitted from combinations of sulphur with alkalies, earths and metals.

This species of air is now called sulphurated hydrogen gas.

Hepatic mercurial ore, a mineral of a reddish, or reddish brown, or dark red color. Its streak is dark red, and has some luster. It occurs in compact masses, with an even or fine grained fracture.

Hepatic pyrite, _hepatic_ sulphuret of iron. During the process of decomposition of this ore, by which the sulphur is more or less disengaged, the pyrite is converted, either wholly or in part, into a compact oxyd of iron of a liver brown color; hence its name.

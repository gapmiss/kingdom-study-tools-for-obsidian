# Hill

**HILL**, _noun_ \[Latin collis.\]

**1.** A natural elevation of land, or a mass of earth rising above the common level of the surrounding land; an eminence. A _hill_ is less than a mountain, but of no definite magnitude, and is sometimes applied to a mountain. Jerusalem is seated on two hills. Rome stood on seven hills.

**2.** A cluster of plants, and the earth raised about them; as a _hill_ of maiz or potatoes.

**HILL**, _verb transitive_ To raise earth about plants; to raise a little mass of earth. Farmers in New England _hill_ their maiz in July.

**HILL**ing is generally the third hoeing.

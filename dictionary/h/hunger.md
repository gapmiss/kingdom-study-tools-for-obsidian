# Hunger

**HUN'GER**, _noun_

**1.** An uneasy sensation occasioned by the want of food; a craving of food by the stomach; craving appetite. _hunger_ is not merely want of food, for persons when sick, may abstain long from eating without _hunger_ or an appetite for food. _hunger_ therefore is the pain or uneasiness of the stomach of a healthy person, when too long destitute of food.

**2.** Any strong or eager desire.

For _hunger_ of my gold I die.

**HUN'GER**, _verb intransitive_ To feel the pain or uneasiness which is occasioned by long abstinence from food; to crave food.

**1.** To desire with great eagerness; to long for.

Blessed are they that _hunger_ and thirst after righteousness. [[Matthew 5#6|Matthew 5:6]].

**HUN'GER**, _verb transitive_ To famish. \[Not in use.\]

# Halcyon

**HAL'CYON**, _noun_ hal'shon. \[Latin halcyon; Gr. a king-fisher.\]

The name anciently given to the king-fisher, otherwise called alcedo; a bird that was said to lay her eggs in nests, on rocks near the sea, during the calm weather about the winter solstice. Hence,

**HAL'CYON**, _adjective_ Calm; quiet; peaceful; undisturbed; happy. _halcyon_ days were seven days before and as many after the winter solstice, when the weather was calm. Hence by _halcyon_ days are now understood days of peace and tranquility.

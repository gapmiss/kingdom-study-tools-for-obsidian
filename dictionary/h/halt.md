# Halt

**HALT**, _verb intransitive_

**1.** To stop in walking; to hold. In military affairs, the true sense is retained, to stop in a march. The army halted at noon.

**2.** To limp; that is, to stop with lameness.

**3.** To hesitate; to stand in doubt whether to proceed, or what to do.

How long _halt_ ye between two opinions? [[1 Kings 18#21|1 Kings 18:21]].

**4.** To fail; to falter; as a halting sonnet.

**HALT**, _verb transitive_ To stop; to cause to cease marching; a military term. The general halted his troops for refreshment.

**HALT**, _adjective_ Lame; that is, holding or stopping in walking.

Bring hither the poor, the maimed, the _halt_ and the blind. [[Luke 14#21|Luke 14:21]].

**HALT**, _noun_ A stopping; a stop in marching.

The troops made a _halt_ at the bridge.

**1.** The act of limping.

# Her

**HER**, pronounced hur, an adjective, or pronominal adjective of the third person. \[Latin suus.\]

**1.** Belonging to a female; as _her_ face; _her_ head.

**2.** It is used before neuter nouns in personification.

Wisdom's ways are ways of pleasantness, and all _her_ paths are peace. [[Proverbs 3#15|Proverbs 3:15]].

**HER** is also used as a pronoun or substitute for a female in the objective case, after a verb or preposition.

She gave also to _her_ husband with _her_ and he did eat. [[Genesis 3#6|Genesis 3:6]].

**HER**s is primarily the objective or genitive case, denoting something that belongs to a female. But it stands as a substitute in the nominative or objective case.

And what his fortune wanted, hers could mend.

**HER**e hers stands for _her_ fortune, but it must be considered as the nominative to could mend. I will take back my own book and give you hers. Here hers is the object after give.

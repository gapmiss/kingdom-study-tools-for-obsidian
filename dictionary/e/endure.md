# Endure

**ENDU'RE**, _verb transitive_ \[Latin durus, duro.\]

**1.** To last; to continue in the same state without perishing; to remain; to abide.

The Lord shall _endure_ forever. [[Psalms 9#7|Psalms 9:7]].

He shall hold it \[his house\] fast, but it shall not _endure_ [[Job 8#15|Job 8:15]].

**2.** To bear; to brook; to suffer without resistance, or without yielding.

How can I _endure_ to see the evil that shall come to my people? [[Esther 8#6|Esther 8:6]].

Can thy heart _endure_ or thy hands be strong? [[Ezekiel 22#14|Ezekiel 22:14]].

**ENDU'RE**, _verb transitive_ To bear; to sustain; to support without breaking or yielding to force or pressure. Metals _endure_ a certain degree of heat without melting.

Both were of shining steel, and wrought so pure.

As might the strokes of two such arms _endure_

**1.** To bear with patience; to bear without opposition or sinking under the pressure.

Therefore, I _endure_ all things for the elect's sake. [[2 Timothy 2#3|2 Timothy 2:3]].

If ye _endure_ chastening, God dealeth with you as with sons. [[Hebrews 12#7|Hebrews 12:7]].

**2.** To undergo; to sustain.

I wish to die, yet dare not death _endure_

**3.** To continue in. \[Not used.\]

# Emaciate

**EMA'CIATE**, _verb intransitive_ \[Latin emacio, from maceo, or macer, lean; Gr. small; Eng. meager, meek.\] To lose flesh gradually; to become lean by pining with sorrow, or by loss of appetite or other cause; to waste away, as flesh; to decay in flesh.

**EMA'CIATE**, _verb transitive_ To cause to lose flesh gradually; to waste the flesh and reduce to leanness.

Sorrow, anxiety, want of appetite, and disease, often _emaciate_ the most robust bodies.

**EMA'CIATE**, _adjective_ Thin; wasted.

# Enfilade

**ENFILA'DE**, _noun_ \[Latin filum.\] A line or straight passage; or the situation of a place which may be seen or scoured with shot all the length of a line, or in the direction of a line.

**ENFILA'DE**, _verb transitive_ \[from the noun.\] To pierce, scour or rake with shot, in the direction of a line, or through the whole length of a line.

In conducting approaches at a siege, care should be taken that the trenches be not enfiladed.

In a position to _enfilade_ the works at Fort Isle.

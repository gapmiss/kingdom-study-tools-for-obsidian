# Exonerate

**EXON'ERATE**, _verb transitive_ egzon'erate. \[Latin exonero; ex and onero, to load, onus, a load.\]

**1.** To unload; to disburden.

The vessels _exonerate_ themselves into a common duct.

But more generally, in a figurative sense.

**2.** To cast off, as a charge or as blame resting on one; to clear of something that lies upon the character as an imputation; as, to _exonerate_ one's self from blame, or from the charge of avarice.

**3.** To cast off, as an obligation, debt or duty; to discharge of responsibility or liability; as, a surety exonerates himself by producing a man in court.

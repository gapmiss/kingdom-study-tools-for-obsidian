# Establish

**ESTAB'LISH**, _verb transitive_ \[Latin stabilio; Heb. to set, fix, _establish_ \]

**1.** To set and fix firmly or unalterably; to settle permanently.

I will _establish_ my covenant with him for an everlasting covenant. [[Genesis 17#7|Genesis 17:7]].

**2.** To found permanently; to erect and fix or settle; as, to _establish_ a colony or an empire.

**3.** To enact or decree by authority and for permanence; to ordain; to appoint; as, to _establish_ laws, regulations, institutions, rules, ordinances, etc.

**4.** To settle or fix; to confirm; as, to _establish_ a person, society or corporation, in possessions or privileges.

**5.** To make firm; to confirm; to ratify what has been previously set or made.

Do we then make void the law through faith?

God forbid; yea, we _establish_ the law. [[Romans 3#31|Romans 3:31]].

**6.** To settle or fix what is wavering, doubtful or weak; to confirm.

So were the churches established in the faith. [[Acts 16#5|Acts 16:5]].

To the end he may _establish_ your hearts unblamable in

holiness. l Thess.3.

**7.** To confirm; to fulfill; to make good.

Establish thy word to thy servant. [[Psalms 119#90|Psalms 119:90]]

**8.** To set up in the place of another and confirm.

Who go about to _establish_ their own righteousness. [[Romans 10#3|Romans 10:3]].

# Espy

**ESPY**', _verb intransitive_ \[Latin specio.\]

**1.** To see at a distance; to have the first sight of a thing remove. Seamen _espy_ land as they approach it.

**2.** To see or discover something intended to be hid, or in a degree concealed and not very visible; as, to _espy_ a man in a crowd, or a thief in a wood.

**3.** To discover unexpectedly.

As one of them opened his sack, he espied his money. [[Genesis 42#27|Genesis 42:27]].

**4.** To inspect narrowly; to examine and make discoveries.

Moses sent me to _espy_ out the land, and I brought him word again. [[Joshua 14#7|Joshua 14:7]].

**ESPY'**, _verb intransitive_ To look narrowly; to look about; to watch.

Stand by the way and _espy_ [[Jeremiah 48#19|Jeremiah 48:19]].

\[This word is often pronounced spy, which see.\]

**ESPY'**, _noun_ A spy; a scout.

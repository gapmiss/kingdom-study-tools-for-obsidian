# Espouse

**ESPOUSE**, _verb transitive_ espouz'. \[Latin spondeo, sponsus, the letter n, in the latter, must be casual, or the modern languages have lost the letter. The former is most probable; in which case, spondeo was primarily spodeo, sposus.\]

**1.** To betroth.

When as his mother Mary was espoused to Joseph. [[Matthew 1#18|Matthew 1:18]].

**2.** To betroth; to promise or engage in marriage, by contract in writing, or by some pledge; as, the king espoused his daughter to a foreign prince. Usually and properly followed by to, rather than with.

**3.** To marry; to wed.

**4.** To unite intimately or indissolubly.

I have espoused you to one husband, that I may present you as a chaste virgin to Christ. [[2 Corinthians 11#2|2 Corinthians 11:2]].

**5.** To embrace; to take to one's self, with a view to maintain; as, to _espouse_ the quarrel of another; to _espouse_ a cause.

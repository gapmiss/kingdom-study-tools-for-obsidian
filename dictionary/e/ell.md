# Ell

**ELL**, _noun_ \[Latin ulna.\] A measure of different lengths in different countries, used chiefly for measuring cloth. The ells chiefly used in Great Britain are the English and Flemish. The English _ell_ is three feet and nine inches, or a yard and a quarter. The Flemish _ell_ is 27 inches, or three quarters of a yard. The English is to the Flemish as five to three. In Scotland, an _ell_ is 37 2/10 English inches.

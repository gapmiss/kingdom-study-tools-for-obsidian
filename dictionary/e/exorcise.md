# Exorcise

**EX'ORCISE**, _verb intransitive_ s as z. \[Gr. to adjure, to bind by oath, an oath.\]

**1.** To adjure by some holy name; but chiefly, to expel evil spirits by conjurations, prayers and ceremonies. To _exorcise_ a person, is to expel from him the evil spirit supposed to possess him. To _exorcise_ a demon or evil spirit, is to cast him out or drive him from a person, by prayers or other ceremonies.

**2.** To purify from unclean spirits by adjurations and ceremonies; to deliver from the influence of malignant spirits or demons; as, to _exorcise_ a bed or a house.

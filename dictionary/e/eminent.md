# Eminent

**EM'INENT**, _adjective_ \[Latin eminens, from emineo.\]

**1.** High; lofty; as an _eminent_ place. [[Ezekiel 16#24|Ezekiel 16:24]].

**2.** Exalted in rank; high in office; dignified; distinguished. Princes hold _eminent_ stations in society, as do ministers, judges and legislators.

**3.** High in public estimation; conspicuous; distinguished above others; remarkable; as an _eminent_ historian or poet; an _eminent_ scholar. Burke was an _eminent_ orator; Watts and Cowper were _eminent_ for their piety.

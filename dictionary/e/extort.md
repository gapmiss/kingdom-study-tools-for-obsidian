# Extort

**EXTORT**', _verb transitive_ \[Latin extortus, from extorqueo, to wrest from; ex and torqueo, to twist.\]

**1.** To draw from by force or compulsion; to wrest or wring from by physical force, by menace, duress, violence, authority, or by an illegal means. Conquerors _extort_ contributions from the vanquished; tyrannical princes _extort_ money from their subjects; officers often _extort_ illegal fees; confessions of guilt are extorted by the rack. A promise extorted by duress is not binding.

**2.** To gain by violence or oppression.

**EXTORT'**, _verb intransitive_ To practice extortion.

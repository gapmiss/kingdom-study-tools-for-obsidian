# Emit

**EMIT**', _verb transitive_ \[Latin emitto; e and mitto, to send.\]

**1.** To send forth; to throw or give out; as, fire emits heat and smoke; boiling water emits steam; the sun and moon _emit_ light; animal bodies _emit_ perspirable matter; putrescent substances _emit_ offensive or noxious exhalations.

**2.** To let fly; to discharge; to dart or shoot; as, to _emit_ an arrow. \[Unusual.\]

**3.** To issue forth, as an order or decree. \[Unusual.\]

**4.** To issue, as notes or bills of credit; to print, and send into circulation. The United States have once emitted treasury notes.

No state shall _emit_ bills of credit.

# Extend

**EXTEND**', _verb transitive_ \[Latin extendo; ex and tendo, teneo.\]

**1.** To stretch in any direction; to carry forward, or continue in length, as a line; to spread in breadth; to expand or dilate in size. The word is particularly applied to length and breadth. We _extend_ lines in surveying; we _extend_ roads, limits, bounds; we _extend_ metal plates by hammering.

**2.** To stretch; to reach forth; as, to _extend_ the arm of hand.

**3.** To spread; to expand; to enlarge; to widen; as, to _extend_ the capacities, or intellectual powers; to _extend_ the sphere of usefulness; to _extend_ commerce.

**4.** To continue; to prolong; as, to _extend_ the time of payment; to _extend_ the season of trial.

**5.** To communicate; to bestow on; to use or exercise towards.

He hath extended mercy to me before the king. [[Ezra 7#28|Ezra 7:28]].

**6.** To impart; to yield or give.

I will _extend_ peace to her like a river. [[Isaiah 66#12|Isaiah 66:12]].

**7.** In law, to value lands taken by a writ of extent in satisfaction of a debt; or to levy on lands, as an execution.

The execution was delivered to the sheriff, who extended the same on certain real estate.

**EXTEND'**, _verb intransitive_ To stretch; to reach; to be continued in length or breadth. The state of Massachusetts extends west to the border of the state of New York. Connecticut river extends from Canada to the sound. How far will your argument or proposition extend? Let our charities _extend_ to the heathen.

# Evacuate

**EVAC'UATE**, _verb transitive_ \[Latin evacuo; e and vacuus, from vaco, to empty. See [[Vacant]].\]

**1.** To make empty; to free from any thing contained; as, to _evacuate_ the church.

**2.** To throw out; to eject; to void; to discharge; as, to _evacuate_ dark-colored matter from the bowels. Hence,

**3.** To empty; to free from contents, or to diminish the quantity contained; as, to _evacuate_ the bowels; to _evacuate_ the vessels by bleeding.

**4.** To quit; to withdraw from a place. The British army evacuated the city of New York, November 25, 1783.

**5.** To make void; to nullify; as, to _evacuate_ a marriage or any contract. \[In this sense, vacate is now generally used.\]

# Intemperance

**INTEM'PERANCE**, _noun_ \[Latin intemperantia.\]

**1.** In a general sense, want of moderation or due restraint; excess in any kind of action or indulgence; any exertion of body or mind, or any indulgence of appetites or passions which is injurious to the person or contrary to morality; as _intemperance_ in study or in labor, in eating or drinking, or in any other gratification. Hence, appropriately and emphatically,

**2.** Habitual indulgence in drinking spirituous liquors, with or without intoxication.

Should a foreign army land on our shores, to levy such a tax upon us as _intemperance_ levies--no mortal power could resist the swelling tide of indignation that would overwhelm it.

# Imbody

**IMBOD'Y**, _verb transitive_ \[in and body.\] To form into a body; to invest with matter; to make corporeal; as, to _imbody_ the soul or spirit.

An opening cloud reveals

A heavenly form, imbodied and array'd

With robes of light.

**1.** To form into a body, collection or system; as, to _imbody_ the laws of a state in a code.

**2.** To bring into a band, company, regiment, brigade, army, or other regular assemblage; to collect; as, to embody the forces of a nation.

Then Clausus came, who led a numerous band

Of troops imbodied.

**IMBOD'Y**, _verb intransitive_ To unite in a body, mass or collection; to coalesce.

# Imbitter

**IMBIT'TER**, _verb transitive_ \[in and bitter.\] To make bitter.

**1.** To make unhappy or grievous; to render distressing. The sins of youth often _imbitter_ old age. Grief imbitters our enjoyments.

**2.** To exasperate; to make more severe, poignant or painful. The sorrows of true penitence are imbittered by a sense of our ingratitude to our Almighty Benefactor.

**3.** To exasperate; to render more violent or malignant; as, to _imbitter_ enmity, anger, rage, passion, etc.

# Intense

**INTENSE**, _adjective_ intens'. \[Latin intensus, from intendo, to stretch.\]

**1.** Literally, strained, stretched; hence, very close, strict, as when the mind is fixed or bent on a particular subject; as, _intense_ study or application; _intense_ thought.

**2.** Raised to a high degree; violent; vehement; as _intense_ heat.

**3.** Very severe or keen; as _intense_ cold.

**4.** Vehement; ardent; as _intense_ phrases in language.

**5.** Extreme in degree.

The doctrine of the atonement supposes that the sins of men were so laid on Christ, that his sufferings were inconceivably _intense_ and overwhelming.

**6.** Kept on the stretch; anxiously attentive; opposed to remiss.

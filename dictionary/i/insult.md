# Insult

**IN'SULT**, _noun_ \[Latin insultus, from insilio, to leap on; in and salio, to leap.\]

**1.** The act of leaping on. \[Little used.\]

**2.** Any gross abuse offered to another, either by words or actions; act or speech of insolence or contempt.

The ruthless sneer that _insult_ adds to grief.

**INSULT'**, _verb transitive_ \[Latin insulto. See the Noun.\]

To treat with gross abuse, insolence or contempt, by words or actions; as, to call a man a coward or a liar, or to sneer at him, is to _insult_ him.

To _insult_ over, to triumph over with insolence and contempt.

**INSULT'**, _verb intransitive_ To behave with insolent triumph.

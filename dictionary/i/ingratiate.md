# Ingratiate

**INGRA'TIATE**, _verb transitive_ ingra'shate. \[Latin in and gratia, favor.\]

**1.** To commend one's self to another's good will, confidence or kindness. It is always used as a reciprocal verb, and followed by with, before the person whose favor is sought. Ministers and courtiers _ingratiate_ themselves with their sovereign. Demagogues _ingratiate_ themselves with the populace.

**2.** To recommend; to render easy; used of things.

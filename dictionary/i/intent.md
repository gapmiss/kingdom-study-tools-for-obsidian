# Intent

**INTENT**', _adjective_ \[Latin intentus, from intendo. See [[Intend]].\]

Literally, having the mind strained or bent on an object; hence, fixed closely; sedulously applied; eager in pursuit of an object; anxiously diligent; formerly with to, but now with on; as _intent_ on business or pleasure; _intent_ on the acquisition of science.

Be _intent_ and solicitous to take up the meaning of the speaker--

**INTENT'**, _noun_ Literally, the stretching of the mind towards an object; hence, a design; a purpose; intention; meaning; drift; aim; applied to persons or things.

The principal _intent_ of Scripture is to deliver the laws of duties supernatural.

I ask therefore, for what _intent_ ye have sent for me? [[Acts 10#29|Acts 10:29]].

To all intents, in all senses; whatever may be designed.

He was miserable to all intents and purposes.

# Invasion

**INVA'SION**, _noun_ s as z. \[Latin invasio, from invado. See [[Invade]].\]

**1.** A hostile entrance into the possessions of another; particularly, the entrance of a hostile army into a country for the purpose of conquest or plunder, or the attack of a military force. The north of England and south of Scotland were for centuries subject to _invasion_ each from the other. The _invasion_ of England by William the Norman, was in 1066.

**2.** An attack on the rights of another; infringement or violation.

**3.** Attack of a disease; as the _invasion_ of the plague, in Egypt.

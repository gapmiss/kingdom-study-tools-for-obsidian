# Involve

**INVOLVE**, _verb transitive_ involv'. \[Latin involvo; in and volvo, to roll, Eng. to wallow.\]

**1.** To envelop; to cover with surrounding matter; as, to _involve_ one in smoke or dust.

**2.** To envelop in any thing which exists on all sides; as, to _involve_ in darkness or obscurity.

**3.** To imply; to comprise. To be and not to be at the same time, involves a contradiction.

**4.** To entwist; to join; to connect.

He knows his end with mine involved.

**5.** To take in; to catch; to conjoin.

_The gathering number, as it moves along,_

**INVOLVE**s a vast involuntary throng.

# Inability

**INABIL'ITY**, _noun_ \[Latin inhabilis; in and habilis.\]

**1.** Want of sufficient physical power or strength; as the _inability_ of a man to raise an arm or a leg.

**2.** Want of adequate means; as an _inability_ to purchase a farm, or to fit out a ship.

**3.** Want of moral power. Moral _inability_ is considered to be want of inclination, disposition or will, or a deep-rooted aversion to act, and therefore improperly so called.

Moral _inability_ aggravates our guilt.

**4.** Want of intellectual strength or force; as an _inability_ to comprehend a mathematical demonstration.

**5.** Want of knowledge or skill; as an _inability_ to read or write.

# Interchange

**INTERCHANGE**, _verb transitive_ \[inter and change.\]

**1.** To put each in the place of the other; to give and take mutually; to exchange; to reciprocate; as, to _interchange_ places; to _interchange_ cares or duties.

I shall interchange

My waned state of Henry's regal crown.

**2.** To succeed alternately.

**IN'TERCHANGE**, _noun_ Mutual change, each giving and receiving; exchange; permutation of commodities; barter; as the _interchange_ of commodities between New York and Liverpool.

**1.** Alternate succession; as the _interchange_ of light and darkness.

Sweet interchange

Of hill and valley, rivers, woods and plains.

**2.** A mutual giving and receiving; reciprocation; as an _interchange_ of civilities or kind offices.

# Indifferent

**INDIF'FERENT**, _adjective_ \[Latin indifferens.\]

**1.** Neutral; not inclined to one side, party or thing more than to another.

Cato knows neither of them,

Indifferent in his choice to sleep or die.

**2.** Unconcerned; feeling no interest, anxiety or care respecting any thing. It seems to be impossible that a rational being should be _indifferent_ to the means of obtaining endless happiness.

It was a remarkable law of Solon, that any person who, in the commotions of the republic, remained neuter, or an _indifferent_ spectator of the contending parties, should be condemned to perpetual banishment.

**3.** Having no influence or preponderating weight; having no difference that gives a preference. It is _indifferent_ which road we take.

**4.** Neutral, as to good or evil. Things in themselves _indifferent_ may be rendered evil by the prohibition of law.

**5.** Impartial; disinterested; as an _indifferent_ judge, juror or arbitrator.

**6.** Passable; of a middling state or quality; neither good, nor the worst; as _indifferent_ writing or paper.

Indifferent, used adverbially, as _indifferent_ honest, is ungrammatical and vulgar.

# Ingraft

**INGR'AFT**, _verb transitive_ \[in and graff. The original word is ingraff or graff, but it is corrupted beyond recovery.\]

**1.** To insert a cion of one tree or plant into another for propagation; as, to _ingraft_ the cion of an apple-tree on a pear-tree, as its stock; to _ingraft_ a peach on a plum.

**2.** To propagate by incision.

**3.** To plant or introduce something foreign into that which is native, for the purpose of propagation.

This fellow would _ingraft_ a foreign name

Upon our stock.

**4.** To set or fix deep and firm.

Ingrafted love he bears to Caesar.

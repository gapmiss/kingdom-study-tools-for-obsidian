# Inn

**INN**, _noun_ \[Heb. To dwell or to pitch a tent.\]

**1.** A house for the lodging and entertainment of travelers. In America, it is often a tavern, where liquors are furnished for travelers and others.

There was no room for them in the _inn_ [[Luke 2#7|Luke 2:7]].

**2.** In England, a college of municipal or common law professors and students; formerly, the town-house of a nobleman, bishop or other distinguished personage, in which he resided when he attended the court.

**INN**s of court, colleges in which students of law reside and are instructed. The principal are the Inner Temple, the Middle Temple, Lincoln's _inn_ and Gray's _inn_

**INN**s of chancery, colleges in which young students formerly began their law studies. These are now occupied chiefly by attorneys, solicitors, etc.

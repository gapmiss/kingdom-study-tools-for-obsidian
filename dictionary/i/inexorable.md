# Inexorable

**INEX'ORABLE**, _adjective_ \[Latin inexorabilis; in and exorabilis, from exoro, to entreat; ex and oro, to pray.\]

**1.** Not to be persuaded or moved by entreaty or prayer; too firm and determined in purpose to yield to supplication; as an _inexorable_ prince or tyrant; an _inexorable_ judge.

**2.** Unyielding; that cannot be made to bend.

Inexorable equality of laws.

# Almoner

**AL'MONER**, _noun_ \[See [[Alms]].\]

An officer whose duty is to distribute charity or alms. By the ancient canons, every monastery was to dispose of a tenth of its income in alms to the poor, and all bishops were obliged to keep an _almoner_ This title is sometimes given to a chaplain; as, the _almoner_ of a ship or regiment.

The Lord _almoner_ or Lord High _almoner_ in England, is an ecclesiastical officer, generally a bishop, who has the forfeiture of al deodands, and the goods of self-murderers, which he is to distribute to the poor.

The Grand _almoner_ in France, is the first ecclesiastical dignitary, and has the superintendence of hospitals.

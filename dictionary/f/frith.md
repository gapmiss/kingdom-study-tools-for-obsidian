# Frith

**FRITH**, _noun_ \[Latin freetum; Gr. from to pass over, or to pass; properly, a passage, a narrow channel that is passable or passed.\]

**1.** A narrow passage of the sea; a strait. It is used for the opening of a river into the sea; as the _frith_ of Forth, or of Clyde.

**2.** A kind of wear for catching fish.

**FRITH**, _noun_

**1.** A forest; a woody place.

**2.** A small field taken out of a common.

\[Not used in America.\]

# Forecast

**FOREC'AST**, _verb transitive_

**1.** To foresee; to provide against.

It is wisdom to _forecast_ consequences.

**2.** To scheme; to plan before execution.

He shall _forecast_ his devices against the strong holds. [[Daniel 11#24|Daniel 11:24]].

**3.** To adjust; contrive or appoint beforehand

The time so well _forecast_

**FOREC'AST**, _verb intransitive_ To form a scheme previously; to contrive beforehand.

Forecasting how his foe he might annoy.

**FO'REC'AST**, _noun_ Previous contrivance; foresight, or the antecedent determination proceeding from it; as a man of little _forecast_

# Firm

**FIRM**, _adjective_ ferm. \[Latin firmus. This is the root of Latin ferrum, iron.\]

**1.** Properly, fixed; hence, applied to the matter of bodies, it signifies closely compressed; compact; hard; solid; as _firm_ flesh; _firm_ muscles; some species of wood are more _firm_ than others; a cloth of _firm_ texture.

**2.** Fixed; steady; constant; stable; unshaken; not easily moved; as a _firm_ believer; a _firm_ friend; a _firm_ adherent or supporter; a _firm_ man, or a man of _firm_ resolution.

**3.** Solid; not giving way; opposed to fluid; as _firm_ land.

**FIRM**, _noun_ ferm. A partnership or house; or the name or title under which a company transact business; as the _firm_ of Hope \_ Co.

**FIRM**, _verb transitive_ ferm. \[Latin firmo.\] To fix; to settle; to confirm; to establish.

And Jove has firm'd it with an awful nod.

This word is rarely used, except in poetry. In prose, we use confirm.

# Founder

**FOUND'ER**, n

**1.** One that founds, establishes and erects; one that lays a foundation; as the _founder_ of a temple or city.

**2.** One who begins; an author; one from whom any thing originates; as the _founder_ of a sect of philosophers; the _founder_ of a family or race.

**3.** One who endows; one who furnishes a permanent fund for the support of an institution; as the _founder_ of a college or hospital.

**4.** A caster; one who casts metals in various forms; as a _founder_ of cannon, belles, hardware, printing types, etc.

**FOUND'ER**, _verb intransitive_

**1.** In seamen's language, to fill or be filled and sink, as a ship.

**2.** To fail; to miscarry.

**3.** To trip; to fell.

**FOUND'ER**, _verb transitive_ To cause internal inflammation and great soreness in the feet of a horse, so as to disable or lame him.

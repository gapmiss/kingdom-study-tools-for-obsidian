# Finely

**FI'NELY**, _adverb_

**1.** In minute parts; as a substance _finely_ pulverized.

**2.** To a thin or sharp edge; as an instrument _finely_ sharpened.

**3.** Gaily; handsomely; beautifully; with elegance and taste. she was _finely_ attired.

**4.** With elegance or beauty.

Plutarch says very _finely_ that a man should not allow himself to hate even his enemies; for it you indulge this passion on some occasions, it will rise of itself in others.

**5.** With advantage; very favorably; as a house or garden _finely_ situated.

**6.** Nicely; delicately; as a stuff _finely_ wrought.

**7.** Purely; completely.

**8.** By way of irony, wretchedly; in a manner deserving of contemptuous notice. He is _finely_ caught in his own snare.

# Flee

**FLEE**, _verb intransitive_

**1.** To run with rapidity, as from danger; to attempt to escape; to hasten from danger or expected evil. The enemy fled at the first fire.

Arise, take the young child and his mother, and _flee_ into Egypt. [[Matthew 2#13|Matthew 2:13]].

**2.** To depart; to leave; to hasten away.

Resist the devil, and he will _flee_ from you. [[James 4#7|James 4:7]].

**3.** To avoid; to keep at a distance from.

**FLEE** fornication; _flee_ from idolatry. [[1 Corinthians 6#10|1 Corinthians 6:10]].

To _flee_ the question or from the question, in legislation, is said of a legislator who, when a question is to be put to the house, leaves his seat to avoid the dilemma of voting against his conscience, or giving an unpopular vote. In the phrases in which this verb appears to be transitive, there is really an ellipsis.

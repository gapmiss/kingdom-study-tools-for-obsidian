# Ferry

**FER'RY**, _verb transitive_ \[Latin fero; allied to bear.\]

To carry or transport over a river, strait or other water, in a boat. We _ferry_ men, horses, carriages, over rivers, for a moderate fee or price called fare or ferriage.

**FER'RY**, _verb intransitive_ To pass over water in a boat.

**FER'RY**, _noun_

**1.** A boat or small vessel in which passengers and goods are conveyed over rivers or other narrow waters; sometimes called a wherry. This application of the word is, I believe, entirely obsolete, at least in America.

**2.** The place or passage where boats pass over water to convey passengers.

**3.** The right of transporting passengers over a lake or stream. _adjective_ B. owns the _ferry_ at Windsor. \[In New England, this word is used in the two latter senses.\]

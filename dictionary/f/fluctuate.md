# Fluctuate

**FLUC'TUATE**, _verb intransitive_ \[Latin fluctuo, from fluctus, a wave, from fluo, to flow.\]

**1.** To move as a wave; to roll hither and thither; to wave; as a fluctuating field of air.

**2.** To float backward and forward, as on waves.

**3.** To move now in one direction and now in another; to be wavering or unsteady. Public opinion often fluctuates. Men often _fluctuate_ between different parties and opinions. Hence,

**4.** To be irresolute or undetermined.

**5.** To rise and fall; to be in an unsettled state; to experience sudden vicissitudes. The funds or the prices of stocks _fluctuate_ with the events of the day.

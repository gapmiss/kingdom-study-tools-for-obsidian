# Forage

**FOR'AGE**, _noun_ \[Latin voro.\]

**1.** Food of any kind for horses and cattle, as grass; pasture, hay, corn and oats.

**2.** The act of providing _forage_

Col. Mawhood completed his _forage_ unmolested.

If the _forage_ is to be made at a distance from the camp -

**3.** Search for provisions; the act of feeding abroad.

**FOR'AGE**, _verb intransitive_

**1.** To collect food for horses and cattle, by wandering about and feeding or stripping the country.

**2.** To wander far; to rove. _obsolete_

**3.** To ravage; to feed on spoil.

**FOR'AGE**, _verb transitive_ To strip of provisions for horses, etc.

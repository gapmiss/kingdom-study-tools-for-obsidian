# Falsify

**FALS'IFY**, _verb transitive_

**1.** To counterfeit; to forge; to make something false, or in imitation of that which is true; as, to _falsify_ coin.

The Irish bards use to _falsify_ every thing.

**2.** To disprove; to prove to be false; as, to _falsify_ a record.

**3.** To violate; to break by falsehood; as, to _falsify_ one's faith or word.

**4.** To show to be unsound, insufficient or not proof. \[Not in use.\]

His ample shield is falsified.

**FALS'IFY**, _verb intransitive_ To tell lies; to violate the truth.

It is universally unlawful to lie and _falsify_

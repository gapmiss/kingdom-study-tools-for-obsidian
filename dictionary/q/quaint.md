# Quaint

**QUAINT**, _adjective_ \[The latter word would lead us to refer _quaint_ to the Latin accinctus, ready, but Skinner thinks it more probably from comptus, neat, well dressed.\]

**1.** Nice; scrupulously and superfluously exact; having petty elegance; as a _quaint_ phrase; a _quaint_ fashion.

To show how _quaint_ an orator you are.

**2.** Subtle; artful. _obsolete_

**3.** Fine-spun; artfully framed.

**4.** Affected; as _quaint_ fopperies.

**5.** In common use, odd; fanciful; singular; and so used by Chaucer.

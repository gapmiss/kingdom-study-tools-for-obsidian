# Quash

**QUASH**, _verb transitive_ \[Latin quasso, quatio.\]

**1.** Properly, to beat down or beat in pieces; to crush.

The whales against sharp rocks, like reeling vessels, quash'd.

**2.** To crush; to subdue; as, to _quash_ a rebellion.

**3.** In law, to abate, annul, overthrow or make void; as, to _quash_ an indictment. He pays judgment of the writ or declaration that the same may be quashed.

**QUASH**, _verb intransitive_ To be shaken with a noise.

**QUASH**, _noun_ A species of cucurbita; but in America pronounced squash; so called probably from it softness. \[See the Verb.\]
